DRAGONBANE ADVENTURES — CHAPTER 3: OUTSKIRT
(Hub Village — Full Location Key and NPCs)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The village of Outskirt has developed around a crumbling temple ruin from the golden
age of the dragon emperors. Most buildings are simple log cabins or rough timber
houses, constructed in the last decade by adventurers, settlers, and fortune hunters.
Outskirt is a place far away from honor and probity, filled with dreams and
adventure, where every rumor and kind smile may lead you to glory, wealth, and
everlasting fame — or to a sudden and excruciating death.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ARRIVAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

After the opening scene, the player characters will probably head directly to
Outskirt. It takes roughly a day's march to get there.

The vegetation thickens into dense forest as the characters descend toward the
Misty Vale. They can simply follow the remains of the same old path winding its way
through the mountains and down into the forest — no BUSHCRAFT roll needed.

Here and there are weathered milestones protruding from the terrain, some decorated
with stylized archaic crowns. A few kilometers from Outskirt the forest thins out
and opens into cultivated fields.

READ ALOUD: APPROACH TO OUTSKIRT

"The wagon tracks give way to a paved road that cuts through the high grain fields.
Countless smells invade your senses: manure, smoke, and rotting vegetables, but also
freshly baked bread and grilled meat.

The road leads to a settlement surrounded by a palisade, with lots of small houses
with smoking chimneys visible beyond its sharpened logs.

The village gate is closed and flanked by two low but sturdy towers of stone with
crenellations and pointed wooden roofs. Drawing closer, you hear the stern voice of a
man barking at you from one of the towers:

'Halt, strangers! State your names and business or turn back to the forest — this is
a place of peace and decency.'"

The man in the tower is Hardy (location #2a). He wants to know their names and why
they have come to Outskirt. If they answer and assure him they come in peace, the
gate creaks open. If they ask questions, Hardy firmly refers them to Vagnhild at
the Three Stags inn.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
THE SITUATION (GM Overview)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Outskirt is the hub for the adventures that make up The Secret of the Dragon
Emperor. It is where the player characters can upgrade gear, purchase weapons, hone
skills, and recover between excursions. It is also where you plant clues which the
players must find to progress the story.

When the characters arrive, it is important that they quickly learn about the legend
of the dragon emperor's statuette and the sword Um-Durman, so they are drawn into
the adventure. Having gained clues in the opening scene, they will likely ask around
the village. Good information sources:
  - Leanara/Annabella: Secret Sathmog cultist at the Three Stags (see below).
  - Alfilia Shadowleaf: Eledain knight at the Three Stags (see below).
  - Dranath: Mystic at the chapel/temple area (location #9).

THE DRAGON TEMPLE

Outskirt is built around an ancient temple where the dragon emperor Eledain and his
descendants are buried. Little of the building remains today, but in a crypt beneath
the ruined walls rests Um-Durman. Opening the entrance requires the fully assembled
statuette of Eledain, which must be placed in a certain niche. See location #8.

THE DEMON CULTIST — LEANARA

In Outskirt they will meet Leanara, the Sathmog-worshiping demon cultist mentioned
by Master Weatherman in the opening scene. She has received two orders from Azrahel
Koth: to monitor the temple ruin and keep an eye on adventurers visiting Outskirt.

Leanara can usually be found at the Three Stags inn (location #4) where she works
as a maid under the name of Annabella. She communicates with Azrahel Koth through
messages delivered to the Isle of Mist by demonic bats. She has a handful of lesser
cultists at her command, led by the bitter mallard warrior Quasimund.

Leanara's plan: Let the player characters find the statuette pieces and the entrance
to Um-Durman, then take the sword from them and bring it to Azrahel Koth. She does
not reveal her connection to Sathmog, but tries to trick the player characters by
feeding them important information mixed with lies and half-truths.

LEANARA'S PITCH (when she approaches the player characters):

After telling the Legend of Um-Durman (see Chapter 1), she continues:
"I'm part of a sacred organization called the Truth Society, which strives to bring
ancient artifacts and lost knowledge back to humankind. You strike me as capable
adventurers. Would you help me find the lost statuette pieces and open the crypt of
Eledain? Our order is not rich, but we can offer you 50 gold coins per piece — and
the honor of fighting for a good cause.

But beware — an order of dragon-worshiping knights is also looking for Um-Durman.
They seek to reestablish the old draconic empire and enslave everyone living in the
Misty Vale."

If the player characters expose and neutralize Leanara before finding Um-Durman,
Quasimund instead arranges the ambush in the temple area (see location #10).


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RANDOM EVENTS IN OUTSKIRT (D6)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Roll when the characters arrive after an excursion, or whenever you want to liven
things up. Several events are connected to Leanara and her cultists.

1  Demonic Bat. A large bat comes flying from the temple area. With a successful
   AWARENESS roll, a player character notices that something is attached to its
   leg. The creature is one of Leanara's vampire bats on its way to Azrahel Koth
   with a secret message. The characters cannot stop it at this time.

2  Quasimund's Scum. "Oh, lookie here! What fiiine visitors have come to Outskirt!
   I hope you didn't forget to pay the toll?" The characters are surrounded by
   unsavory types led by the mallard Quasimund. They stink of mead and are as many
   as the player characters plus 1. The thugs give up if more than half of them
   are wounded or if given money for mead. They are really testing the characters'
   combat capabilities on orders from Leanara.

3  Troll in the Barn. Pandemonium spreads through the village and a voice shouts
   "Troll in old man Mifaldor's barn!" The next moment the troll is right in front
   of the player characters. It is a huge creature sniffing the air with its
   enormous nostrils. The characters are plunged into combat unless they act
   quickly, but the troll can be placated with a successful PERSUASION roll.
   For stats, see page 97 in the Core Rulebook.

4  Flour Thieves. Agitated shouting is heard from the mill, and soon the miller
   Baryton Chubbycheeks comes huffing and puffing toward the characters. Several
   sacks of his finest flour are missing and he will pay "whatever it takes" to
   get them back. Baryton suspects Quasimund and his goons, but with a successful
   BUSHCRAFT roll, the characters can find tracks leading to the Iron Forest where
   some goblins are feasting on the flour. For goblin stats, see page 90 in the
   Core Rulebook.

5  Dead Messenger. There is a nasty bat carcass on the ground in front of the
   characters, pierced by a crossbow bolt with a small scroll tied to its foot.
   The message is written in obscure runes but can be deciphered with a successful
   LANGUAGES roll: "Newcomers in Outskirt. Purpose unclear. Keeping an eye on
   them." It ends: "All might to Sathmog, our three-tongued liberator." The bat
   was shot down by Guard Captain Hardy.

6  Cowardly Murderers. The characters hear excited cries and clashing swords from
   a dark alley. They find Alfilia Shadowleaf lying on the ground. Three shadows
   are fleeing — they are part of Quasimund's Scum and will fight to the death if
   stopped. If questioned, they proudly admit they serve Sathmog. Alfilia is
   fatally wounded and beyond saving. She manages to gasp: "They are servants of
   Sathmog… all of them… Annabella too…" After a final prayer to Eledain,
   she exhales: "Hail Eledain, master of the immaculate flame!"


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RUMORS IN OUTSKIRT (D12)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Use these to point characters toward adventure sites where statuette pieces can
be found. Many NPCs in Outskirt can relay these rumors.

1   Riddermound. "This side of the Iron Forest there's an unholy place with huge
    stones rising into the sky. Riddermound it's called, named after a dark knight
    who was chained by the gods when the world was young. They say he was buried
    alive in that mound, along with his family, his servants, and all his riches."

2   The Orc Clans. "A human lifetime ago it was the orcs who ran things in the
    Misty Vale. No one knows why they left, but rumor has it that a chieftain named
    Maladûk is gathering the clans to reclaim the valley."

3   Bothild's Lode. "The dwarves of the Kummer Mountains are decent and honest
    folk. It was worse in the old days — during the reign of Bothild the Vile and
    Greedy — when they dug too deep in their miserable mines and unleashed nameless
    horrors on the world. But then again, what treasures they found! Apparently,
    it's all still down there in Bothild's Lode, in the far southwest."

4   Temple of the Purple Flame. "The Magna Woods is a diseased forest, and we all
    know where the malady is coming from — the Temple of the Purple Flame, a
    crumbling remnant of an evil age, where forbidden knowledge and demonic residue
    rest among untold treasures under the overgrown rocks."

5   Tower of Sighs. "There's a disgusting bog about a day's march east of here.
    It's crawling with bloodsucking leeches and insects, and is a hideout for
    bandits. But there are vast riches out there in the sludge, buried in the ruins
    of an ancient watchtower. People around here call it the Tower of Sighs."

6   Oracle Cave. "Did you hear about young Roric? The boy was taken from his farm
    outside Outskirt. The tracks lead to the Iron Forest. People say he was brought
    to the Oracle Cave. The Oracle is ancient and knows all there is to know about
    the Misty Vale, and she's said to be sitting on immense wealth. But now and then
    she demands a human sacrifice. I reckon we'll never see Roric again."

7   Troll's Spire. "When the moon is full you can see a tower glittering like a
    silver spire in the western parts of the valley. A magical tower, no doubt, for
    none has yet found the way there. Some say it's home to an age-old alchemist
    who long ago incurred the wrath of the dragon emperor. She hid from Eledain in
    her tower, where she concocted an elixir that gave her everlasting life."

8   Dead Eyes Cave. "A monstrous beast has crawled down from the mountains and
    attacked Blind Hildi's farm southeast of Outskirt. There are whispers about a
    cave full of torn-out eyes, but also great treasures and powerful weapons from
    ill-fated adventurers who met their fate in the Dead Eyes Cave."

9   Road's End Inn. "There is something strange about Road's End Inn by the edge
    of the Haunted Marshes. Several guests there have disappeared without a trace,
    among them a small child, and most recently the shepherd Antelia. Her family
    is worried sick."

10  The Village of the Day Before. "Seven centuries ago, this valley was home to
    Kato the Curious, a sorcerer who served Emperor Eledain but betrayed his liege.
    Kato's tower and the nearby village of Blackridge, in the far northeast, perished
    in dragon fire when his treachery was discovered. Now he's said to haunt the
    ruins of his tower, which supposedly holds all kinds of treasures. But no one who
    tried to loot the ruins has ever returned."

11  Fort Malus. "Fort Malus was constructed by House Stoneoak near the end of
    Emperor Eledain's reign, to protect the family's most prized possession — the
    Malus Aureus, a tree that bore golden fruit, apples of actual gold! The ruins of
    Fort Malus still stand in the far northwest, probably with thousands of golden
    apples lying at the roots of the old tree."

12  The Isle of Mist. "The island in Mirror Lake at the northern end of the valley
    is a place of death, avoided by all that live and breathe. Stories tell of ancient
    magic — and a crypt brimming with gold and wondrous artifacts. But the island is
    wreathed in demonic fog that sucks the life out of anything that comes near."

1. PALISADE

A crude palisade of sharpened logs winds its way around the settlement. Not very
high — maybe one and a half men tall. Several large gaps can be seen between the
logs.

  - Guards: Patrolled around the clock by three villagers plus the guard captain
    at the South Gate. One villager always mans a tower at the North Gate while
    the others walk the palisade.
  - Random Events #1 and #5 can occur here.


ARMED VILLAGERS (generic stats)
  Movement: 10   Damage Bonus: —   HP: 11   Armor: Leather (1)
  Skills: Evade 8, Awareness 8
  Typical Weapon: Short spear (skill level 10, damage D10)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2A. SOUTH GATE

A low, double-gated entrance flanked by two round towers of stone. A paved road
leads south.

  - Captain Hardy resides in one tower; the other is used as a briefing room and
    gathering place for the guard patrols.
  - Random Events #1 and #5 can occur here.


HARDY — Guard Captain
  A battle-hardened war veteran in his 40s, with shoulder-length hair and a broken
  nose. The strong, silent type, but with a sentimental side that comes out after
  a few mugs of mead at the inn. Sometimes heads out into the wilds to drive off
  goblins, bandits, and other troublemakers, often accompanied by Jory.

  Mov.: 12   Damage Bonus STR: +D4   HP: 16   WP: 14
  Armor: Studded leather and open helmet (3)
  Skills: Awareness 14, Brawling 14, Evade 12, Healing 8
  Abilities: Defensive, Veteran
  Weapons: Broadsword (skill level 14, damage 2D6), heavy crossbow (skill level 12,
           damage 2D8), large shield

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2B. NORTH GATE

A low gateway flanked by two round towers of stone with pointed wooden roofs. A
well-trodden path leads out through the gate and snakes off through the terrain.

  - Lookout: Always a villager standing guard in one tower, always equipped with
    a large horn. Three short horn blasts followed by one long blare = danger.
    During emergencies, guard increases to four — two in each tower.
  - Random Events #1 and #5 can occur here.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3. THE VILLAGE SQUARE

The inn, the smithy, and the shop all face an open space where a weathered statue
rises from the earth — a strange relic from a bygone era, depicting a warrior in
antiquated armor.

  - The Dragon Emperor: Both arms have fallen off, but one can still make out a
    helmet with a horn-like crown. With a successful MYTHS & LEGENDS roll, the
    characters recognize the statue as the dragon emperor Eledain, and notice great
    similarities between the statue and the four-piece statuette on Weatherman's map.
  - Random Events: All random events on page 16 can occur here.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

4. THE THREE STAGS (Inn)

An impressive two-story building with a massive thatched roof and half-timbered
walls. Outside the door hangs a red sign: "The Three Stags — Beer, bed, and food
at heavenly prices." Cheery voices spill out, along with the smell of roasted boar.

  - Common Room: From early morning to well after midnight there are D12+1 villagers
    and visitors sitting in the common room — eating, drinking, gossiping, and
    warming their joints by the great fireplace. Delicious meat stews and homemade
    beer or mead are served at normal inn prices.
  - Lodging: Upstairs there are ten beds divided between two double rooms and a
    dormitory. Prices as per the Core Rulebook.
  - Investigations: An excellent place to seek information about the Misty Vale.
    Clues the characters may have missed elsewhere can be placed here as rumors
    or a merry song.

KEY NPCs HERE:

VAGNHILD — Innkeeper (informal village leader)
  An authoritative but jovial woman in her 40s. Keeps her graying hair in bulky
  braids and wears a thick leather apron. Used to be a mercenary and always carries
  a warhammer on her belt. Acts as informal village leader; coordinates the guards
  along the palisade.

  She welcomes the characters and gives each a tankard of mead on the house. Offers
  to answer questions about the area and can relay rumors about adventure sites.
  Also tells them: large bats have lately been seen flying over the rooftops (Hardy
  has tried to shoot them down); a mystic named Dranath has settled in the temple
  area and is a skillful healer.

  Mov.: 8   Damage Bonus STR: +D4   HP: 12   Armor: Leather (1)
  Skills: Evade 8, Healing 10, Persuasion 14
  Weapons: Light warhammer (skill level 12, damage 2D6)

LEANARA / ANNABELLA — Sathmog Cultist (VILLAIN)
  Leanara has served Sathmog ever since her family was killed by fanatical Eledain
  knights. She is a haggard woman of indeterminate age, but with kind eyes and
  deep dimples. She will gladly lay down her life to thwart the servants of Eledain.

  Works at the Three Stags as a perky, seemingly trustworthy maid named Annabella.
  Will not wait long to contact the player characters. See GM Overview above for
  her pitch and plans.

  Bats: Characters who search the inn and succeed with a SPOT HIDDEN roll will
  find a cage with D3 sleeping bats hanging outside a window on the second floor.
  If they hide and keep an eye on the window, within one shift they will see Leanara
  fetch one of the bats. With a successful SNEAKING roll, they can follow her to the
  temple area, where she releases the bat after attaching a small piece of paper to
  its leg.

  Mov.: 14   Damage Bonus AGL: +D4   HP: 14   WP: 15   Armor: —
  Skills: Brawling 12, Evade 14, Persuasion 12, Sneaking 16
  Abilities: Backstabbing, Fast Footwork, Lightning Fast, Weasel
  Weapons: Dagger (skill level 16, damage D8 + lethal poison potency 14)

QUASIMUND — Leanara's Enforcer (Mallard)
  A failed fortune hunter who regularly frequents the Three Stags. Bitter and violent,
  which tends to come out when he drinks too much mead. Has sold his soul to Sathmog
  for a few pieces of silver and half a keg of mead.

  Mov.: 10   Damage Bonus STR: +D4   HP: 14   WP: 12   Armor: Leather (1)
  Skills: Brawling 14, Evade 14, Sneaking 12
  Abilities: Defensive, Veteran, Weasel
  Weapons: Scimitar (skill level 16, damage 2D6), light crossbow (skill level 15,
           damage 2D6)

QUASIMUND'S SCUM (generic thugs)
  Drunken wrecks of settlers and adventurers who lost everything in pursuit of
  riches. Like Quasimund, they have sold their souls to Sathmog and attack as a
  group, at least equal in number to the player characters.

  Movement: 10   Damage Bonus: —   HP: 12   Armor: —
  Skills: Evade 10, Sneaking 8
  Weapons: Short sword (skill level 14, damage D10), sling (skill level 12, damage D8)

ALFILIA SHADOWLEAF — Keeper of the Immaculate Flame
  Sent by the Keepers of the Immaculate Flame. Looking for the keys to Um-Durman,
  while trying to prevent the sword from ending up in Azrahel Koth's hands.
  Grumpy and quiet, throws suspicious glances at Quasimund and his thugs.

  If the characters gain her trust — showing a statuette piece and succeeding with
  a PERSUASION roll — she can tell them the legend of Um-Durman and provide clues:
  - Sathmog has reawakened Azrahel Koth to find Um-Durman.
  - Azrahel Koth is probably in his crypt beneath Mirror Lake. Seems to be bound
    to that place, but has many spies and servants in the Misty Vale.
  - On the mist-shrouded island in Mirror Lake is the entrance to Azrahel Koth's
    unholy resting place. The island is inaccessible — the mist is demonic and sucks
    the life out of anything that comes near.
  - She suspects it would take powerful anti-demonic magic to break through —
    such as the sword Um-Durman.
  - She is convinced Azrahel Koth has several spies in Outskirt. She has seen
    demonic bats flying over the rooftops and thinks they are being used for secret
    communication between the crypt under the Isle of Mist and someone in Outskirt.

  Mov.: 10   Damage Bonus STR: +D4   HP: 15   WP: 14
  Armor: Plate (6)
  Skills: Brawling 14, Evade 12
  Abilities: Defensive, Veteran
  Weapons: Morningstar (skill level 16, damage 2D6), small shield

DEMONIC BATS (Leanara's swarm)
  Through Azrahel Koth, Leanara commands a swarm of demonically controlled vampiric
  bats. Some are kept in a cage, but most are scattered around Outskirt. The swarm
  arrives two rounds after being telepathically summoned by Leanara. For stats,
  see page 99 in the Rulebook.

5. MASTER ULVAR'S (Shop)

An oblong, windowless log building. On the roof there are goats feeding on unkempt
grass. Above the entrance hangs a grained wooden sign saying "Master Ulvar's" in
curling letters.

  - The shop is run by the proud Master Ulvar with assistance from his indolent and
    disinterested son Jory.
  - Gear: Does not sell weapons, shields, or armor, but most Common items listed in
    chapter 6 of the Rulebook can be purchased here.

MASTER ULVAR — Shopkeeper
  A tall, red-faced man in his 50s with an impressive drooping mustache. Hands the
  size of hams and a booming voice that makes his mustache quiver.

  Mov.: 8   Damage Bonus STR: +D4   HP: 15   Armor: —
  Skills: Brawling 12, Sneaking 8
  Weapons: Long spear (skill level 12, damage 2D8)

JORY — Ulvar's Son
  Jory has little interest in shopkeeping and would rather be a warrior like Hardy —
  according to persistent rumors, Jory's real father. The mother is unknown, but
  there is talk of a half-orcish woman who left for the mountains some years ago.

  Mov.: 12   Damage Bonus STR: +D4   HP: 14   Armor: Leather (1)
  Skills: Evade 12, Sneaking 9
  Weapons: Broadsword (skill level 12, damage 2D6), short bow (skill level 10, damage D10)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

6. SMITHY

A large log building with an open front. The glow of a hearth can be glimpsed
through the smoke, and from the hooks in the ceiling hang all kinds of weapons and
armor pieces. Heavy hammer blows and a melodic, dwarven rumble pour out into the
village square.

  - The smithy is run by Okald and Badinor, dwarven twins from the Kummer Mountains
    who settled in Outskirt after a difficult inheritance dispute.
  - Weapons: All Common and Uncommon weapons, shields, and armor listed in chapter 6
    of the Rulebook can be purchased here. Rare items can be ordered and be ready in
    a week. The twins can also repair damaged weapons and armor, usually for half the
    original purchase price.

OKALD — Extroverted Twin (handles customers)
  A heavily tattooed dwarf woman with a thick braided beard and a formidable
  underbite. Happy and friendly, likes to tell stories of her wild years in the
  mountains.

  Mov.: 8   Damage Bonus STR: +D4   HP: 16   Armor: Leather (1)
  Skills: Crafting 12, Evade 6, Persuasion 10
  Weapons: Handaxe (skill level 15, damage 2D6)

  ERRATA: Okald has skill level 12 in CRAFTING (corrected from original printing).

BADINOR — Surly Twin (works in the smithy)
  Surly and taciturn, feels more comfortable around rocks than living creatures.
  Speaks to minerals in their own language. Nevertheless, a skillful blacksmith
  with the respect of everyone in the village.

  Mov.: 6   Damage Bonus STR: +D4   HP: 14   WP: 15   Armor: Leather (1)
  Skills: Crafting 15, Evade 6
  Abilities: Master Blacksmith
  Weapons: Battleaxe (skill level 13, damage 2D8), light crossbow (skill level 12,
           damage 2D6)

  ERRATA: Badinor has skill level 15 in CRAFTING (corrected from original printing).

7. THE MILL

At the edge of the village is a small windmill whose sails rotate slowly in the
wind. The mill is connected to a log house where piglets, chickens, and carefree
toddlers run around in the fenced yard. An almost irresistible scent of freshly
baked bread surrounds the idyllic place.

  - Mr. and Mrs. Chubbycheeks: Spouses Semolina and Baryton Chubbycheeks run the
    village mill. They are halflings and live in the adjoining cottage with their
    nine children and countless pets.
  - Field Rations: The spouses also run a humble bakery where the characters can buy
    freshly baked bread for only 3 silver coins per daily ration.

SEMOLINA CHUBBYCHEEKS
  An unusually muscular halfling with thick upper arms and a flour-stained apron.
  She keeps the mill wheels turning and produces the flour needed to keep the
  villagers' hunger at bay.

  Movement: 8   Damage Bonus: —   HP: 12   Armor: Leather (1)
  Skills: Evade 12, Healing 9, Sneaking 13
  Weapons: Wooden club (skill level 8, damage D8)

BARYTON CHUBBYCHEEKS
  A plump but elegant halfling with handsome curls and a winning smile. Responsible
  for stock-keeping, sales, and baking the bread. Always stressed, worrying that
  their resources might not be enough to feed their numerous offspring.

  Movement: 8   Damage Bonus: —   HP: 10   Armor: —
  Skills: Evade 14, Persuasion 15, Sneaking 14
  Weapons: Dagger (skill level 12, damage D8)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

8. THE TEMPLE AREA

A hill in the middle of the village with weathered pillars and the remains of a
circular wall. At the crest stands a magnificent but crumbled temple. A peaceful
place that exudes a sense of bygone glory and ancient mystery.

  - The Temple: The circular main building is in disrepair, but the ceiling is
    covered with a huge symbol. A successful MYTHS & LEGENDS roll reveals it is a
    stylized crown of the same kind as those on the milestones in the Misty Vale.

  - Entrance to the Crypt: There is a small niche hewn into one of the wall segments
    around the temple. The characters can find it if they look in exactly the right
    place or succeed with a SPOT HIDDEN roll. Carved into the niche are archaic runes
    which can be read with a successful LANGUAGES roll: "Eledain — Emperor of the
    World — Master of Dragons — Keeper of Um-Durman." At the bottom of the niche is
    a depression. Placing the fully assembled statuette in this depression opens the
    entrance to the hidden chapel (location #10). When that happens, the ground in
    front of the niche collapses with a loud crash and reveals a rough-hewn stairway
    of stone leading down into the dark. A player character standing by the niche
    must succeed with an EVADE roll or fall four meters and take damage.

  - Nocturnal Visits: If the characters visit the temple during nighttime, they will
    see Leanara on the first night. She sneaks into the temple area and releases one
    of her bats, which flutters off with an unpleasant squeak. She then spends about
    an hour searching the area before returning to the Three Stags.

  - Random Events: All random events from the Outskirt table can occur here.

9. DRANATH'S HUT

A simple dwelling with a grass roof and walls of stacked granite. A connecting
garden is surrounded by a low fence. Herbal-smelling smoke billows out of an
opening in the roof.

DRANATH — Mystic
  Dranath is wrinkled as a shriveled prune and does not have a single hair on his
  body. He wears a sack-like garment and goes barefoot regardless of the weather.
  Despite his asceticism, Dranath can be tempted with sweets, and a mug of mead
  is usually enough to loosen his tongue.

  Movement: 8   Damage Bonus: —   HP: 12   WP: 16   Armor: —
  Skills: Animism 16, Healing 15, Languages 13, Myths & Legends 15
  Spells: Sense Magic, Dispel, Protector, Magic Shield, Banish, Ensnaring Roots,
          Lightning Flash, Treat Wound, Heal Wound, Lightning Bolt, Purge
  Weapons: Staff (skill level 12, damage D8)

  - Healing Magic: Dranath can treat the characters' injuries with HEAL WOUND at
    the price of 5 gold coins per power level.

  - Teacher: For a fee of 3 gold coins per shift, Dranath can teach the characters
    his skills and spells.

  - Clues (if the characters ask the right questions and succeed with a PERSUASION
    roll, or offer him mead or sweets):
      > The legend of Um-Durman.
      > A thousand years ago the Misty Vale was ruled by a warlock named Azrahel
        Koth who served the demon prince Sathmog. Azrahel Koth resided in an unholy
        fortress on the mist-shrouded island in Mirror Lake. His remains are said to
        rest in a labyrinthine burial chamber beneath the island.
      > Azrahel Koth was defeated by the hero Eledain with the help of ancient
        dragons. Eledain then declared himself emperor. The temple ruin in Outskirt
        originates from that empire.
      > A mysterious woman is sneaking around the temple area at night, seemingly
        looking for something. Dranath has heard her speak in an ancient language to
        another creature, which responded with a squeak and flew off.

10. THE CRYPT OF UM-DURMAN

A tomb-like shrine with empty torch holders on the walls. The darkness is dense,
but on a stone altar in the middle of the room you can see a jeweled greatsword, a
golden goblet, and an open book. Water from a fountain in the wall pours down into
a small font. Menacing statues of knights in horned great helms are placed in the
corners.

  - Darkness: Pitch black — adventurers will need a light source.

  - Magical Trap: Touching the sword without first reciting the prayer from Eledain's
    prayer book AND taking a sip of water from Eledain's goblet triggers a LIGHTNING
    BOLT at power level 1. Another LIGHTNING BOLT is triggered for each round of
    continued contact until the trap's CHARGE of 20 WP is depleted (each LIGHTNING
    BOLT uses 4 WP as it is indoors).

  - Eledain's Prayer Book: The hefty tome is open at a page with ancient but clear
    letters. Reading them requires a successful LANGUAGES roll: "Hail, Emperor of the
    World — Lord of Light — Punisher of Impurity — Eledain Forever, in the Depths of
    Our Pure Hearts."

  - Eledain's Goblet: A golden goblet decorated with a stylized crown of the kind
    seen elsewhere in the Misty Vale.

  - Um-Durman: The sword on the altar is the much-sought blade Um-Durman — a
    mastercrafted weapon with beautiful ornaments and a jeweled hilt. Um-Durman is a
    greatsword with STR requirement 13, durability 18, and a special version of
    ENCHANT WEAPON with PERMANENCE — whenever the sword hits a dragon or demon, the
    attack automatically counts as a dragon roll.

  - Leanara's Betrayal: If the characters acted on behalf of Leanara — or shared
    information from their investigations with her — she arranges an ambush outside
    the entrance and attacks the moment they emerge from the chapel. She sends a
    swarm of her demonic bats at the group, while Quasimund charges in with his thugs.
    Leanara herself throws at the player character carrying Um-Durman, poisoned
    knife in hand.

  - HEROIC ABILITY: If the player characters retrieve Um-Durman and defeat the
    cultists, each of them is rewarded with a heroic ability of their choice after
    the end of the session.
