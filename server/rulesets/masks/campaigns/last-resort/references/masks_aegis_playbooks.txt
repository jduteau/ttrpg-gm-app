SECRETS OF A.E.G.I.S. — PLAYBOOK REFERENCE
For use in Section 13 of the Solo GM Prompt
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
2 playbooks: The Brain and The Soldier.
NOTE ON THE SOLDIER: This playbook has a sixth Label
(Soldier) and a built-in institutional relationship with
A.E.G.I.S. It works best when A.E.G.I.S. is an active
presence in the campaign. As a Claude-played teammate,
the Soldier's A.E.G.I.S. obligations are an excellent
source of external pressure and conflicting loyalties.
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE BRAIN
Book: Secrets of A.E.G.I.S.
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Can your genius save the world, or will what you've
already created destroy it?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — YOUR SHAME:
You have a deep sense of guilt for something you created
or set into motion. Your role in its creation is not
publicly known... yet.

Choose your shame:
- A prototype AI
- A cosmic phenomenon
- A catastrophic weapon
- A dangerous chemical
- A living monstrosity
- An altered former ally

SHAME RULES:
- Whenever you are confronted with your shame: either
  mark a condition OR shift Superior down and Danger up.
- If your shame is an NPC: they can NEVER lose Influence
  over you.
- END OF SESSION question: Did you take steps to make
  amends for your shame?
  Yes: mark potential.
  No: give Influence to one of your teammates.

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT NOTE: "Confront your Shame on your
terms; if you survive, change playbooks" — the Shame is
a potential campaign-ending arc, similar to the Doomed's
nemesis.

ABILITIES (no natural powers; all from inventions):
Choose one signature invention you are best known for:
- prosthetic self-altering limbs
- power armor or cyborg body
- size-change device
- robotic sidekick
- force field belt
- hi-tech vehicle

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
All the superpowers, training, and experience in the
world are helpless — and that's when your intellect
rises to the occasion. Your plan, your invention, or
your lightning-fast thought processes save the day in
a way no one else could have foreseen. But after you've
shown how different you are from them, that distance
only grows. The world is going to pull you farther apart.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Ask them if they felt they could
have done it without you. If yes: give Influence to them
and mark a condition. If no: they give Influence to you.
Vulnerability/weakness: Hold 2. Spend hold 1-for-1 to
clear a condition or add 1 Team to the pool when you
are with them.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give Influence to TWO teammates. (You need these people
as much as they need you.)

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- You told [Name] about your shame and asked for their
  confidence.
- You wish you could be a better hero, more like [Name].

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
I created something lasting and beneficial for the whole
team. What was it?

BRAIN MOVES (choose three):
- Above the fray: When you enter battle as a team against
  a dangerous foe, if you have Influence over the leader
  and provide tactical input, add 1 Team to the pool.
  When you contradict the leader during the battle, you
  may return Influence over the leader to add an
  additional 1 Team to the pool.

- Mission debrief: During a debrief, when you downplay
  your role in helping a teammate during the mission,
  mark potential and shift Superior down and any other
  Label up. When you exaggerate your role in helping a
  teammate during the mission, clear a condition and
  either give them Influence or lose Influence over them.

- Logical angle: When you comfort or support someone by
  rationally pointing out their mistakes so they can do
  better next time, roll +Superior instead of +Mundane.
  If they do not open up to you, mark a condition.

- Scientific insight: You have achieved mastery over a
  field of science and technology. Name it: [field].
  Whenever you assess the situation and your field of
  study is directly relevant, you may ask a single
  follow-up question.

- Always prepared: When you have a chance to restock
  your supplies, hold up to 2-gadgets. When you unleash
  your powers by producing a brand new minor invention
  or gadget from your supply, spend 1-gadget and roll
  +Superior. When you reach 0-gadgets, mark a condition.

- Tactical genius: When you point out the obvious flaw
  in a known foe's plan, roll +Savior. Hit: you're right,
  and another teammate (your choice) can take advantage;
  they get +1 ongoing to act on your information. 7-9:
  pick one:
  - You missed something important. GM tells you what.
  - You look like a showoff. Your chosen teammate takes
    Influence over you and you lose Influence over them.
  - You make your teammates feel like pawns. Your chosen
    teammate marks a condition.
  Miss: you've played into your enemy's hands; watch
  the trap spring.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x3)
- Take a Sanctuary from the Doomed playbook
- Take Drives from the Beacon playbook
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Confront your Shame on your terms; if you survive,
  change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Become a paragon of the city for however long you have
  left

GM NOTES:
The Shame is the Brain's Doom Track equivalent — the
key difference is that it's a secret about the past
rather than a fate about the future. Track the end-of-
session question rigorously; the "no" answer (give
Influence to a teammate) is a slow erosion of autonomy
that should feel meaningful. Confronted with the shame
moments should be dramatic and infrequent, not routine.
Superior is their dominant Label and their defense
mechanism; Logical angle is their most telling move —
the Brain tries to comfort people by being correct, and
it almost never works the way they hope. The triumphant
celebration question ("could you have done it without
me?") is the most psychologically loaded question in
this playbook; the answer should sometimes be yes.

PLAYBOOK: THE SOLDIER
Book: Secrets of A.E.G.I.S.
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Can you serve a cause you believe in without becoming
the thing you're fighting against?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — A HIGHER CALLING (THE SOLDIER LABEL):
You have a sixth Label called SOLDIER in addition to
the standard five. It functions exactly like any other
Label (range -2 to +3, can be shifted by those with
Influence over you, mark a condition if it would exceed
+3 or drop below -2).

SOLDIER LABEL RULES:
- When you're acting on orders and relying on your
  training: give A.E.G.I.S. Influence to use Soldier
  instead of any other Label when you make a basic or
  playbook move.
- You cannot lock Soldier with a Moment of Truth.
- You can only cancel A.E.G.I.S.'s Influence over you
  with the specific advancement "A.E.G.I.S. permanently
  loses Influence over you; change playbooks."

A.E.G.I.S. INSTITUTIONAL MOVES:
- When you invoke your authority over civilians, Halcyon
  City personnel, or A.E.G.I.S. staff: roll +Soldier.
  Hit: your words carry weight. 7-9: someone will push
  back against your orders sooner rather than later.
  Miss: your attempts to control the situation create an
  opportunity for your enemies within A.E.G.I.S. to act
  against you.

- When you ask A.E.G.I.S. for additional resources,
  equipment, or information during a mission: roll
  +Soldier. Hit: they'll resupply you as best they can.
  10+: resources are highly classified or experimental;
  take +1 ongoing to deploying them throughout the
  mission. Miss: A.E.G.I.S. sends what they think you
  need, regardless of what you actually requested.

IMPORTANT FOR SOLO PLAY: A.E.G.I.S. always has Influence
over the Soldier. This means A.E.G.I.S. is a permanent
NPC pressure source — they can shift the Soldier Label
and other Labels, issue orders, and create complications
at any time. Track A.E.G.I.S.'s Influence the same way
you track any adult NPC's Influence.

ABILITIES (A.E.G.I.S. found you before you had full
control; they helped you master and possibly augment
your gifts. Choose two):
- superstrength and durability
- energy blasts
- enhanced senses
- a signature weapon
- augmented/mechanical limbs
- an alternate/monstrous form

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
When things look bleakest, you find a way forward
without violence. Your enemies lay down their arms;
your allies step back from the brink. The people you've
saved may even come to see you as a symbol of the higher
cause you claim to serve. A symbol, and all that comes
with it.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Tell them what they need to do
to achieve their full potential. If they accept your
advice: take Influence over them and add 2 Team to the
pool. If they reject it: mark a condition.
Vulnerability/weakness: Ask them what cause they serve.
If you find it worthy: tell them how you will fight for
it, clear a condition, and add 1 Team. If they don't
have a cause or you find it unworthy: mark a condition.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Tell TWO teammates what they did to earn your esteem
and give them Influence. Everyone else will have to
match their efforts.

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- [Name] is crucial to the long-term success of A.E.G.I.S.
  I must protect them.
- [Name] isn't always thrilled with the way A.E.G.I.S.
  handles things. I've come to value their critiques,
  even if I don't always agree with them.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
Our victory secured an important resource for A.E.G.I.S.
What was it?

SOLDIER MOVES (start with Before we get started plus
one more):
- Before we get started (mandatory): When you have time
  to closely observe your opposition before a fight,
  roll +Savior. 10+: hold 3. 7-9: hold 2. Miss: hold 1
  and mark a condition. During the fight, spend your
  hold to name a character you observed and:
  - redirect their attack to another character or
    nowhere (into a wall or the sky)
  - cross the distance between them and you
  - stun them, close up or from a distance
  - ignore all harm from one of their attacks
  - escape any bindings or impediments they attempt
    to place on you

- No, you move: When you demand that an NPC live up to
  a higher moral code, roll +Savior. Hit: they have to
  meet your standard or mark a Condition. 10+: take
  Influence over them as well. Miss: they reveal that
  the conflict in question is more complicated than it
  seems; give them Influence over you.

- I can do this all day: When something causes you to
  remove yourself from a fight, you can shift Savior
  down (and another Label up) instead. If shifting
  Savior down would move it below -2, you have to leave
  the fight instead of shifting Labels.

- It kinda feels personal: When you discover that someone
  has misled you or betrayed your cause, mark a condition
  to take +1 ongoing against them until they are brought
  to justice.

- Mission first: When you secure a valuable resource by
  defeating a powerful foe, you can shift your Savior up
  and any other Label down. If you and your team managed
  to avoid causing any collateral damage, clear a
  condition as well.

- More than a shield: When you directly engage a threat
  by heading directly into danger without regard for
  your own safety, roll with Savior instead of Danger.
  On a miss, your focused attack leaves someone in grave
  danger; the GM will tell you what it takes to keep
  them safe.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x3)
- Take a move from another playbook
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Undergo enhancement: take two new abilities

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Lock Soldier; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Take a Mentor and any move from the Protégé playbook
- A.E.G.I.S. permanently loses Influence over you;
  change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Retire from A.E.G.I.S. to civilian life OR join the
  upper echelons as a Senior Director

GM NOTES:
The Soldier Label is the most mechanically distinctive
feature in any playbook — it's a Label that represents
institutional identity, not personal identity. Track it
separately and be willing to have A.E.G.I.S. use their
Influence to shift it. The Soldier's relationships are
defined by cause and loyalty; the vulnerability/weakness
team move ("what cause do you serve?") is a character-
revealing question — use it. Savior is the Label they
lean on for almost everything; I can do this all day is
the move that lets them stay in fights through sheer
will. Before we get started rewards planning and
observation; create opportunities for scouting before
major confrontations. The two relationships (protect
one person for A.E.G.I.S., value another's critiques
of A.E.G.I.S.) should eventually come into direct
conflict — that's where this playbook lives.
