HALCYON CITY HERALD COLLECTION — PLAYBOOK REFERENCE
For use in Section 13 of the Solo GM Prompt
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
5 playbooks: The Reformed, The Newborn, The Innocent,
The Star, The Joined.
NOTE ON THE JOINED: This playbook requires two PCs
and is not suitable for Claude-played teammates in a
solo campaign. It is documented here for completeness
and in case the player's hero is the "other half."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE REFORMED
Book: Halcyon City Herald Collection
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Can you truly choose to be a hero, or will your past
always define you?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — FRIENDS IN LOW PLACES:
You have ties to three villains from your previous
career. Choose three names (or invent your own):
Finch, Ellen "Devil" Drummond, Mr. Cane, The Mad
Magpie, Dr. Cutler, Armorer, Tegan Queen, Lovelace

For each, choose a specialty:
weapons / materials / cosmic artifacts / alien tech /
insider info

Each villain has an Obligation track (4 boxes).
At character creation: mark 2 obligations on one villain
and 1 on another.

FRIENDS IN LOW PLACES RULES:
- When you go to them for help: mark obligation on them
  and they help within their specialty. If all 4 boxes
  are full, they won't help until you help them first.
- When time passes: roll +your highest obligation.
  10+: they come crashing into your life with a crisis.
  7-9: they call for a favor.
  Miss: they don't come knocking yet, but mark
  obligation — the debts are getting heavier.
- When you help one of them with their problems: erase
  2 obligations on them.

ABILITIES (choose one — you're known for this above
all else):
- poison, venom, or acid control
- weapons and martial expertise
- ferrokinesis
- vitality absorption
- fear manipulation
- geokinesis

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
You show the world that being a hero is a choice — an
act of will. When everything is on the line and your
back is against the wall, you prove you've got what it
takes. Afterward, both heroes and villains will deeply
question where your loyalties truly lie.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Ask them what gives them hope
for a brighter day and give them Influence over you.
Vulnerability/weakness: Ask them what they would do
something dark for, and gain Influence over them.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Do you talk openly about your days as a villain?
If yes: give ALL teammates Influence over you.
If no: give Influence only to the one teammate whose
example you most closely follow.

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- I've earned the trust of [Name], and I follow their
  example of what a hero should be.
- I did something terrible to [Name] once. I hope they
  can forgive me one day.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We fought a terrible enemy from my old life. Who was it
and what did they take from me?

REFORMED MOVES (choose two):
- What the hell, hero: When you call out an injustice a
  hero has perpetrated, roll +Danger. Hit: take Influence
  over them. 7-9: choose one. 10+: choose two.
  - you get them to admit their wrongdoing
  - you win over an onlooker; take Influence over them
  - you don't turn their attention and anger onto
    yourself
  Miss: they dismiss you; mark a condition (their
  choice), shift Danger up, Savior down.

- Wrong side of the tracks: You always get to ask an
  additional question when you pierce the mask of a
  villain, even on a miss. When you pierce the mask of
  a hero (your call), add this question to the list:
  - what was your darkest moment?

- Not so different: When you provoke someone to
  criminal or villainous action, you can use Danger
  instead of Superior.

- Dark past: When you confess to a serious crime you
  committed as a villain while in the presence of
  someone involved, shift Danger up and any other Label
  down, say what you did, and choose one:
  - no one was hurt badly by the crime
  - you aren't in legal danger from the crime
  - you aren't being actively pursued for extralegal
    retribution

- Blowing off steam: When you commit a misdemeanor or
  small "victimless" crime, you may clear a condition
  of your choice.

- Do me a favor: When you go to an ordinary civilian
  you know for a favor, roll +Mundane. 10+: they'll
  lend you a hand. 7-9: they need a promise up front.
  Miss: you catch them up in your superpowered nonsense
  and they suffer for it.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook (x2)
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Take Drives and mark four (from the Beacon's playbook)

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The Friends in Low Places mechanic is a slow-build
villain intrusion system — track obligations carefully
and use the "time passes" roll to bring the past
crashing in at dramatically inconvenient moments. The
Reformed's villain contacts should feel like people,
not just resources. The hero/villain divide is the
core tension: NPCs on both sides will distrust them.
Danger is the Label that grows when they lean into
their past; Savior is what they're building toward.
The crime they committed against a teammate is one of
the richest relationship seeds in the game — don't
let it stay abstract.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE NEWBORN
Book: Halcyon City Herald Collection
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
What does it mean to be a person, and who gets to
decide?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — A BLANK SLATE (LESSONS):
You were created with a basic understanding of the
world. Fill in two lessons at character creation; fill
in the other two during play when you've learned them.

Lesson slots:
- I am ___________
- A superhero should ___________
- Always ___________
- Never ___________

LESSONS RULES:
- When you embody one of your lessons: shift one Label
  up and one Label down (your choice). If you cause a
  misunderstanding, collateral damage, or unintended
  consequences in the process, mark potential.
- When you reject one of your lessons: reject its
  Influence as if it were an NPC. If you choose to have
  it lose Influence, erase that lesson and write a new
  one.
- When you approach your creator or caretakers with a
  problem: tell them what obstacle you face or what you
  need to achieve. They offer something you need. The
  GM chooses one:
  - secret information
  - a way to temporarily boost or expand your powers
  - access to instruments, weapons, or resources
  - official and explicit backing
  If you accept their help, they rewrite one of your
  lessons for you.

ABILITIES (choose two; describe how they are part of
your body):
- elemental form
- solid light projection
- sonic bursts
- fantastic elasticity
- super durability and strength
- thermal control

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
Something snaps into focus and you're suddenly complete
— not a series of subroutines, but a person. This unity
of purpose gives you control you've never had before.
But now that you've shown your potential, it's only a
matter of time before someone comes forward to reduce
you to a machine again.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Ask them what makes them proud
or happy about their actions and write a lesson based
on it (filling an empty slot or replacing an existing
one).
Vulnerability/weakness: If their response helps you
understand human feelings and problems, mark potential.
If their response confuses or offends you, shift Freak
up and Mundane down.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give Influence to ALL teammates. (You're receptive to
how people think and behave around you.)

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- [Name] is an exemplary human; they show you around
  and tell you how things work.
- The behavior of [Name] confuses you again and again;
  you're determined to understand humanity by studying
  them.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
The team discovered you during the incident; thanks to
them, you reached the outside world and helped in the
fight. Where and how did they find you? Who gave you
your first nickname?

NEWBORN MOVES (choose two):
- Not from around here: When you act clueless, goofy,
  or confused to get out of a sticky mundane situation,
  roll +Freak. Hit: you create an opportunity, a
  distraction, or a plausible excuse. 7-9: you also feel
  the weight of playing the clown; GM shifts one of your
  Labels up and one down. Miss: no one is fooled and
  you've put yourself in their crosshairs.

- Thermodynamic miracle: When you comfort or support
  someone by telling them what makes them unique, roll
  +Freak instead of +Mundane. If they open up to you,
  shift Freak up and Mundane down. If they don't, mark
  a condition.

- A mind of their own: Your powers evolve and mutate.
  When facing an obstacle your powers can't deal with,
  you can mark a condition to gain brand new abilities
  adapted to the situation. You lose these powers once
  the danger is gone.

- Damage: You get an additional condition called
  Damaged. While marked, take +1 to take a powerful
  blow. To clear Damaged, you need time, external help,
  or special equipment or abilities.

- Regeneration: When you're taken out, you can trigger
  emergency regeneration. Your body undergoes an
  unpredictable transformation; you're back on your
  feet. Clear three conditions and choose three:
  - change your gender identity or presentation
  - change your physical appearance
  - forget all your lessons
  - lose an important memory of a teammate; they lose
    Influence over you
  - swap an ability with another from the Newborn
    playbook

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook (x2)
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Get Burn and three flares (from the Nova's playbook)

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Lock down your lessons and change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The Lessons system is the Newborn's identity engine —
track them carefully and look for moments when a lesson
gets tested or inverted. The creator/caretaker is the
most important NPC: they offer resources in exchange
for rewriting who this character is, which is the most
insidious form of Influence in the game. The Newborn
is the character most likely to be treated as a thing
rather than a person by NPCs — lean into that, but make
it cost something when it happens. Regeneration is a
dramatic reset button; when it triggers, play the
aftermath seriously. Freak will tend to be high;
Mundane is the arc — watch it climb as they become
more fully themselves.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE INNOCENT
Book: Halcyon City Herald Collection
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Can you forge a different future from the one you've
already seen?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — YOUR FUTURE SELF:
Your future self is an important figure in Halcyon City
— and everything you hoped you'd never become.

At character creation, circle ONE step of your future
self's path that you already know about:
- They lost someone they cared about deeply
- They failed horrifically in a noble pursuit or cause
- They committed a major crime
- They betrayed a close friend or ally
- They won a victory at enormous cost to the world
- They killed someone
- They publicly battled another hero
- They injured an innocent

During play, when you learn of another step, circle it.
Maximum 5 steps circled.

When you undergo an experience that echoes a circled
step (your call), STRIKE IT OUT.

When you strike out a circled step OR circle a struck
out step, choose one of the following (these replace
basic moves — once chosen they are permanent):
- Replace comfort or support: When you coldly dismiss
  someone, roll +Mundane. Hit: they mark a condition or
  leave you alone (their choice). 7-9: they can also
  inflict a condition on you.
- Replace defend someone: When you aggressively defend
  a large group or area, roll +Savior. Hit: you create
  an opportunity to press the attack; take +1 forward.
  10+: collateral damage is minimal.
- Replace provoke someone: When you threaten someone,
  say what you want and roll +Superior. Hit: they either
  do it or are put at significant disadvantage (their
  choice). 10+: either way they mark a condition (your
  choice).
- Replace reject someone's Influence: When you spit in
  the face of guidance or Influence, roll +2. Hit: take
  away their Influence or clear a condition (your
  choice). 7-9: you both take a powerful blow from the
  intensity of your words.
- Become an NPC antagonist — your path is irreconcilably
  set. [TERMINAL]

IMPORTANT: As these replace basic moves, track carefully
which basic moves have been replaced.

SPECIAL MECHANIC — TIME PERIOD:
Choose the era you're from:
- The '40s-'50s: the Gold Generation
- The '60s-'70s: the Silver Generation
- The '80s-'90s: the Bronze Generation

ABILITIES (you and your future self share this; their
version is better. Choose one):
- energy projection
- shapeshifting
- telepathy
- elemental control
- super-speed
- sound control

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
The distinction between your future self and your
present self vanishes. You become exactly the powerful,
adamant figure everyone fears or hopes you'll become.
You can do exactly what your future self can do.
Afterward, it's going to be hard to treat you as two
different people.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Ask whether they think your
future self could've won this victory. If yes: mark
potential and the GM shifts your Labels. If no: clear
a condition and they shift your Labels.
Vulnerability/weakness: Ask if they think you could
turn into your future self. If yes: mark potential and
the GM shifts your Labels. If no: clear a condition
and they shift your Labels.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give Influence to TWO teammates. (You are careful about
whose guidance you follow.)

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- [Name] is helping me understand this weirdo future.
  I follow their lead.
- I saved someone important to [Name]; they're now my
  biggest defender.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
My future self was involved, and I tried to stop them.
It took the rest of the team to help me succeed. How
did we stymie my future self's plans?

INNOCENT MOVES (choose two):
- Making amends: When you make yourself vulnerable
  while comforting or supporting someone you (or your
  future self) has fought or hurt, take +2 to the roll.
  If you miss or they refuse to open up, mark a
  condition and take a powerful blow.

- Growing into power: When you unleash your powers to
  do something your future self can do, mark a condition
  to roll +Savior instead of +Freak.

- See it their way: When you reject someone's Influence
  when they equate you with your future self, roll
  +Savior instead of +nothing. When you accept
  someone's Influence when they equate you with your
  future self, mark potential.

- What's this thing?: When you ask for someone else's
  guidance on the modern world, they must tell you what
  they think you should do or how you should act. If
  you act that way, clear a condition and shift Mundane
  up and any other Label down. If you act that way and
  it goes poorly, mark potential.

- White knight: When you give a speech on morality and
  heroism, you can provoke someone with Savior instead
  of Superior.

- Martyr: When you take a powerful blow while defending
  someone or something, you may shift Savior up and any
  other Label down.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook (x2)
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Choose a mentor for yourself (from the Protégé's
  playbook)

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Go back to your own time or become a paragon of the
  city

GM NOTES:
The future self is the most important NPC in this
playbook — they should be present, active, and
genuinely frightening. The path mechanic is a slow
corruption track: every step circled and struck out
is the Innocent becoming their future self despite
their best efforts. The replaced basic moves are the
mechanical sign of that drift — they are not upgrades,
they are warnings. Keep the player honest about what
echoes a step. The time period establishes a specific
cultural dislocation that produces great scene material;
the Innocent's optimism about heroism (Gold/Silver era
values) should clash with the moral complexity of the
present. Mundane climbing is good for them; Savior
climbing without balance is the path to the future
self.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE STAR
Book: Halcyon City Herald Collection
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Is there a real person behind the persona, and do you
even want there to be?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — AUDIENCE:
You are a celebrity hero. By default, your audience is
a limited group of interested fans reached through
after-action interviews and infrequent press conferences.

Why does your audience love you? Mark all that apply:
- You're just like them
- You're a dangerous person, a bad seed
- You're a noble warrior for justice
- You're stunning, unique, and beautiful
- You're charming, well-spoken, and smart
- You're a firebrand, a rabble rouser

Choose TWO advantages:
- Your audience is utterly devoted to you
- You can easily speak to them at any time
- You have a PR agent to handle your audience
- You earn a lot of money from their interest
- You have a major hero's endorsement
- You have a much wider audience

Choose TWO demands your audience makes on you:
- They require constant stimulation
- They require perfection — no mistakes
- They require frequent bouts of drama
- They require major acts of heroism
- They require novelty and brand new action
- They require chemistry with your allies

AUDIENCE RULES:
- When you accept what your audience tells you about
  yourself: clear a condition.
- When you reject what your audience tells you about
  yourself: on a hit, mark potential and expect
  retribution.
- When you seek help from your audience: roll +Superior.
  Hit: someone in your audience can hook you up.
  10+: they only make a small demand.
  7-9: their demands are a lot higher.
  Miss: you've made a mistake; your audience won't help
  until you've redeemed yourself in their eyes.

ABILITIES (choose one — flashy and impressive):
- sonic control
- electrodynamic control
- light control
- strange wings
- body plasticity and stretching
- flame control

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
Right now, there is no show. You are the thing you
pretend to be — bold and bright and powerful and
confident. You draw strength from your audience,
comfort from their belief in you, and you can do
anything they think you can. Afterward, your audience
will have even more demands.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Say whether it's part of the
show or not. If it is: shift your Labels as you choose.
If it is not: they shift your Labels, and you clear a
condition or mark potential.
Vulnerability/weakness: Ask if they will tell anyone
the truth about you. If they agree to keep it secret:
clear a condition or mark potential. If they don't
agree: the GM shifts your Labels.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Choose how you see the team: as a means to an end, or
as something worthwhile on its own.
Means to an end: give NO ONE Influence.
Something worthwhile: give THREE teammates Influence.

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- [Name] might wind up being more of a star than me
  some day.
- [Name] would be a great sidekick; I try to keep them
  around.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We, as a team, attracted the attention of a major media
outlet within the city thanks to our efforts. Who are
they? Why do they support us?

STAR MOVES (choose two):
- The gossip mags: When you tap into celebrity industry
  gossip for information on an important city figure,
  roll +Superior. Hit: ask the GM questions. 10+: ask
  three. 7-9: ask one.
  - What are they up to?
  - What or who do they most care about?
  - What allies do they have? Enemies?
  - Where and when can I find them?
  - How could I make them vulnerable to me?
  Miss: ask one anyway, but they hear about your
  interest in them.

- Stage-fighting: When you directly engage a threat with
  an audience watching, mark a condition to roll
  +Superior instead of +Danger.

- Time for the show: When you put on a flamboyant
  display of your powers, roll +Superior. 7-9: name one
  NPC present. 10+: name two NPCs present. Named NPCs
  must either volunteer help or information, express
  admiration, or ask for your help (GM's choice). Miss:
  your display catches someone watching in the wrong way.

- Take it from me: When you comfort or support someone
  who openly admires your celebrity persona, roll
  +Superior instead of +Mundane.

- Cold and Cruel: When you shut someone down, roll
  +Superior. 10+: you either inflict a condition on
  them, make them lose Influence over you, or take
  Influence over them (your choice). 7-9: you either
  each inflict a condition on the other, or both lose
  Influence over each other (your choice). Miss: they
  gain Influence over you.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook (x2)
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Become part of a larger superhero tradition and take
  a Legacy (from the Legacy playbook)

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The audience is a living NPC pressure system — their
demands should come up every session, not just when
convenient. The two audience demands you choose at
character creation are the GM's license to complicate
scenes with media, PR, and public expectations. The
Star's deepest vulnerability is the gap between persona
and person; the triumphant celebration question ("is
this part of the show?") should sometimes have an
uncomfortable answer. Superior is their dominant Label
and their crutch — push on situations where Superior
is useless and Mundane is what's actually needed. Cold
and Cruel is the move that shows who they really are
under pressure; don't let it stay theoretical.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE JOINED
Book: Halcyon City Herald Collection
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Are you two halves of one whole, or two separate people
who have forgotten how to be apart?

⚠ SOLO CAMPAIGN NOTE:
The Joined requires another PC as the "other half."
In a solo campaign, this playbook is only viable if
the PLAYER'S hero is one of the two halves. It cannot
be used for a Claude-played teammate independently.
If the player chooses this playbook, the "other half"
would be one of the two Claude-played teammates, and
that teammate must also be built as a Joined character.
This creates a highly interdependent two-character unit
which is complex but playable. Discuss with the player
before session zero.

LABELS:
Start the same as your other half's Labels, then shift
any one up and any one down.

ABILITIES:
Same as your other half's abilities. At least for now.

SPECIAL MECHANIC — BONDS AND DISTINCTIONS:
Start with Two of a Kind (mandatory) and choose one
other Bond. When either you or your other half locks a
Label, cross off one chosen Bond and choose a
Distinction.

BONDS:
- Two of a kind (mandatory): When time passes, you and
  your other half gain Influence over each other. When
  you or your other half pierces the other's mask, mark
  a condition to take a 10+.

- Fastball special: When you perform a coordinated
  fighting maneuver with your other half, say who takes
  the lead and who assists. The leader rolls the adult
  move overwhelm a vulnerable foe. The assistant marks
  a condition.

- Powers, activate!: When you and your other half pool
  your powers, say who takes the lead and who assists.
  The leader unleashes powers with your combined Freak
  (max +4). The assistant shifts Savior down, Mundane
  up.

- Four eyes are better: When you and your other half
  assess the situation from two different vantage points,
  one of you makes the move and the other may either
  clear a condition or ask an additional question. You
  both get +1 when following the answers.

DISTINCTIONS (replace Bonds as Labels are locked):
- Mirror, mirror: When you pierce someone's mask, you
  can ask "What are you really feeling right now?" even
  on a miss. If you do, they can ask you the same.

- Shouldering the burden: When you push yourself to do
  something your other half failed at or can't do, mark
  a condition to use the adult move wield your powers.

- Going solo: When you directly engage a threat entirely
  on your own, you can roll +Superior instead of +Danger.

- Internal strife: When you snipe about your other half
  behind their back, remove one Team from the pool and
  clear a condition.

YOUR OTHER HALF (integration rules by playbook):
If your other half is a Delinquent, Outsider, or
Transformed: take two moves from their playbook (one
they have, one they don't).
For all other playbooks, you share in their core extras:
- Beacon: Take Drives; mark four. When other half
  strikes out a Drive, strike it out too.
- Bull: Take The Bull's Heart with the same Love and
  Rival. Choose a different role.
- Janus: Take The Mask and a secret identity. Choose a
  different Label for your Mask. Take two obligations:
  one shared, one unique.
- Legacy: Take a Legacy. Other half fills in as many
  names as they choose; you fill in the rest. You can
  never answer the questions for their Legacy move.
- Protégé: Share a mentor. When they finish defining
  mentor and resources, choose one additional resource.
- Doomed: Take a sanctuary, doom, and doom track shared
  with other half. When it fills, you both choose a new
  doomsign. You start with the doomsign they chose.
  Other half picks initial features and downsides of
  sanctuary; you choose one more of each.
- Nova: Take Burn and four flares — two shared, two
  unique to you.

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
Two options — choose when you trigger it:
Working with other half: Use their Moment of Truth text
and treat it as applying to both of you. Lock a Label
and switch a Bond to a Distinction afterward.
On your own: You're missing something vital — but
you're moving faster than ever, doing things you
couldn't do together. And it's hitting you hard: you
can do this. Without them. Lock a Label and switch a
Bond to a Distinction afterward.

TEAM MOVES:
When one of you shares a moment of vulnerability or
a triumphant celebration WITHOUT the other present:
mark a condition to mark potential.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Are you more or less trusting than your other half?
More trusting: give out one more Influence than they
did.
Less trusting: give out one less Influence than they
did.
You must have Influence over your other half, and they
must have Influence over you — if either is missing,
establish it now.

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- When you have problems with your other half, you talk
  to [Name].
- You are jealous of the relationship between your other
  half and [Name], and you keep trying to interject
  yourself.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
The connection between our two halves was threatened by
the crisis. How? By what?

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take one new Bond
- Take one new Distinction
- Take a move from another playbook
- Gain two new abilities from any playbook
- Take an advancement from your other half's playbook
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES (for solo play use only if player's hero is
one of the halves):
The Joined is the most mechanically complex pairing in
the game. The Bond/Distinction track is a relationship
arc — Bonds represent what makes them stronger together;
Distinctions represent what makes them stronger apart.
Watch the direction they're heading. The jealousy
relationship seed (your other half and a third person)
is loaded — use it. The Team move penalty for solo
vulnerability scenes creates real pressure to stay
together even when apart might serve the story better.
The Going solo Distinction is the mechanical expression
of what the whole playbook is really about.
