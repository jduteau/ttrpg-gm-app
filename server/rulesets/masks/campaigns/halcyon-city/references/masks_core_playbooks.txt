MASKS: A NEW GENERATION — CORE PLAYBOOK REFERENCE
For use in Section 13 of the Solo GM Prompt
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
All 10 core playbooks. Starting Labels are flexible —
the playbook sets a base array and the player adds +1
wherever they choose at character creation.
Base array for all core playbooks: Danger -2/-1/0/+1/+2/+3,
same for all five Labels; the player marks the starting
values on the sheet. Key distinctions are in the moves,
setup, and special mechanics.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE BEACON
Book: Masks: A New Generation (Core)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Why do you belong here when you have no real powers?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — DRIVES:
Choose four Drives at the start of play. When you fulfill
a marked Drive, strike it out and choose one: mark
potential, clear a condition, or take Influence over
someone involved. When all four marked Drives are struck
out, choose four new ones. When ALL drives on the list
are struck out, change playbooks, retire, or become a
paragon.

Drive list:
- lead the team successfully in battle
- kiss someone dangerous
- punch someone you probably shouldn't
- help a teammate when they most need you
- take down a threat all on your own
- outperform an adult hero
- pull off a ridiculous stunt
- save a teammate's life
- get drunk or high with a teammate
- drive a fantastical vehicle
- get a new costume
- get a new hero name
- earn the respect of a hero you admire
- make out with a teammate
- punch out a teammate
- break up with someone
- stop a fight with calm words
- tell someone your true feelings for them
- travel to an incredible place (or time)
- reject someone who tells you "you shouldn't be here"

ABILITIES (choose two — minor powers or skills with gear):
- bow and trick arrows
- swords
- camouflage and stealth
- martial arts
- phasing
- acrobatics

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
You show them exactly why you belong here. You do any
one thing, take out any one enemy, no matter how insane
or ridiculous — because that's you. Their jaws drop when
you're done. Pulling off a stunt like this tends to bring
unwanted attention and a dangerous reputation.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Tell them how they're awesome and
add 1 Team. If they tell you how you're awesome in return,
add another Team.
Vulnerability/weakness: Ask them to confirm or deny that
you should be here. If they confirm: mark potential and
give them Influence. If they deny: mark Angry and shift
one Label up and one down (your choice).

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give Influence to THREE teammates. (You are so excited
to be here.)

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- [Name] is awesome, and you take every chance to hang
  out with them.
- You've got to prove yourself to [Name] before you feel
  like a real hero.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We found signs that this incident was just the start of
something bigger. What were the signs?

BEACON MOVES (choose two):
- Straight. Up. Creepin'.: When you scope out a person
  or place, roll +Mundane. 10+: ask two. 7-9: ask one.
  Questions: best way in/out? / what happened here
  recently? / what here is worth grabbing? / who or what
  is not what they seem? / whose place is this?
  Miss: you find yourself in over your head.

- No powers and not nearly enough training: You're always
  picking up new gear. When you pick up a new piece of
  gear, write it in as a new ability (if the line is
  empty). The first time you use each piece of gear to
  directly engage, unleash powers, or defend someone, you
  can roll +Mundane instead of the normal Label.

- Won't let you down: When you help a teammate, you can
  spend 2 from the Team pool to add +2 to their roll
  (instead of the usual +1 for 1 Team).

- Pretty much a superhero: When you bring up your hero
  name to someone important for the first time, roll
  +Savior. Hit: they've heard of you; say which exploit
  and which Label they assign. 7-9: GM tells you something
  else they've heard and picks a second Label they assign.
  Miss: they don't take you seriously or mistrust you.

- C'mon, Lucky: You have a pet/smaller companion. Detail
  it. Choose three basic moves — it helps you with those.
  Take +1 to those moves when your pet could help. If the
  pet gets hurt, treat it as taking a powerful blow.

- Suck it, Domitian: When you stand strong while
  dramatically under fire, roll +Savior instead of +Danger
  to directly engage a threat.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook (x3)
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The Beacon's identity pressure lives in the gap between
enthusiasm and competence. Push on whether they truly
belong. NPCs who doubt them are gold. The Drives give
the player agency — track which are struck out and look
for opportunities to fulfill them dramatically. Their
high Mundane makes them the emotional anchor of the team;
lean into that when they least expect it.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE BULL
Book: Masks: A New Generation (Core)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Can you ever stop being a weapon and become a person?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — THE BULL'S HEART:
You always have exactly one Love and one Rival (named
at character creation). You can change them at any time;
give the new subject Influence over you. Take +1 ongoing
to any action that impresses your Love or frustrates
your Rival.

Choose one role you fulfill for your Love or Rival:
- Defender: When you leap to defend your Love or Rival
  in battle, roll +Danger instead of +Savior.
- Friend: When you comfort or support your Love or Rival,
  mark potential on a hit. When they comfort/support you,
  mark potential when they roll a hit.
- Listener: When you pierce the mask of your Love or
  Rival, you can always let them ask you a question to
  ask them an additional question in turn, even on a miss.
  These extra questions don't have to be on the list.
- Enabler: When you provoke your Love or Rival, roll
  +Danger if trying to provoke them to rash action.

ABILITIES:
Superhumanly tough, incredibly strong, uniquely skilled
at fighting. Decide how each manifests.

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
You let loose — all the pent-up strength and rage and
glee — and you break whatever stands in your way. You are
a walking demolition crew. Nothing can stop you. But now
the people who changed you know exactly where to find
you.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Make them your Love or Rival
immediately to mark potential. If they're already your
Love or Rival, take Influence over them and mark
potential.
Vulnerability/weakness: Give them Influence and hold 2.
Spend that hold to help them as if it were Team in the
pool.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give Influence ONLY to your Love and your Rival.
(You're selective about who you let in.)

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- [Name] is your Love. You've opened up to them about
  the worst parts of your past.
- [Name] is your Rival. They tried to control you at a
  crucial moment.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We defeated a dangerous enemy. Who or what was it?

BULL MOVES (choose two):
- Thick and thin skinned: Whenever you have Angry marked,
  take +1 ongoing to unleash your powers.

- You've got a head you don't need: When you provoke
  someone with obvious threats and shows of force, roll
  +Danger instead of +Superior.

- Punch everyone: Whenever you charge into a fight
  without hedging your bets, you can shift your Danger
  up and any other Label down.

- There when it matters: When you defend someone, on a
  hit you can hold 1 instead of choosing from the list.
  Spend your hold when they are in danger later to arrive
  on the scene ready to help.

- In a china shop: When you directly engage a threat,
  you can cause significant collateral damage to your
  environment to choose an additional option, even on
  a miss.

- Physics? What physics?: When you unleash your powers
  to barrel through an insurmountable barrier, roll
  +Danger instead of +Freak.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook (x2)
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Choose another two roles for The Bull's Heart

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The Bull's entire emotional architecture is built around
two people — Love and Rival. Every scene that involves
either is automatically charged. The people who changed
them are a perpetual threat clock. Push on their
inability to be gentle or strategic. Conditions hit hard
here: Angry gives them a mechanical boost (Thick and thin
skinned) that can make them reckless. Their low Influence
count means the Influence they do give carries enormous
weight.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE DELINQUENT
Book: Masks: A New Generation (Core)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Are you the rebel facade, or is there a hero underneath?

ABILITIES (choose two — messy, deceiving, or frustrating):
- teleportation
- tricks/illusions
- gadgetry and hacking
- psychic weapons
- emotion control
- power negation

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
You show them what you really are — the hero underneath
the rebel facade, or the one who can make the hard
choices heroes can't. You do whatever it takes. But once
you've shown what you really are, there's no going back
to playing the clown.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Ask them if they think you're
cool. If yes: give them Influence and take Influence over
them. If no: mark a condition or spurn them immediately.
Either way (if teammate): add 1 Team.
Vulnerability/weakness: Give them Influence and ask who
they'd like you to be. Mark potential if you show them
that person. (If teammate: add 1 Team regardless.)

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give Influence to THREE teammates. (You care way more
than you let on.)

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- You keep trying to impress [Name] with your antics.
- You and [Name] pulled an awesome (if illegal) stunt
  together.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We totally broke some major rules to win the fight. What
rules did we break? Whose rules were they?

DELINQUENT MOVES (choose three):
- Mary Contrary: When someone tries to pierce your mask,
  comfort or support you, or provoke you, you can
  interfere. Roll +Superior. Hit: they take -2 on their
  roll. 10+: you also take Influence over them OR clear
  a condition. Miss: they get a 10+ no matter what they
  rolled and you mark a condition of their choice.

- I don't care what you think!: Whenever you reject
  others' Influence, add +2 to your roll.

- Team? What team?: When you use Team selfishly, clear
  a condition or mark potential. The first time in a
  session you use Team to help a teammate, take +1
  forward.

- Criminal mind: When you assess the situation, you can
  always ask one of the following, even on a miss:
  - what here is useful or valuable to me?
  - how could I best infuriate or provoke [name]?
  - what's the best way in/way past?

- Troublemaker: When you help a teammate through
  destructive, criminal, or rule-breaking actions, you
  can give them +2 instead of +1 when you spend a Team.

- Are you watching closely?: When you mislead, distract,
  or trick someone, roll +Superior. Hit: they are fooled,
  at least for a moment. 10+: choose three. 7-9: choose
  two.
  - you get an opportunity
  - you expose a weakness or flaw
  - you confuse them for some time
  - you avoid further entanglement
  Miss: you're hopelessly embroiled; mark a condition.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook (x2)
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Add +1 to any two Labels

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The Delinquent is most interesting when genuinely torn
between the rebel act and the hero underneath. NPCs who
believe in them despite everything are as important as
authority figures who write them off. Their Team? What
team? move creates interesting friction — reward selfish
Team use with consequences, not just mechanics. The "cool"
question in triumphant celebration is a loaded gun: say
no occasionally.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE DOOMED
Book: Masks: A New Generation (Core)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
How do you live fully when you know how it ends?

SPECIAL MECHANICS — DOOM TRACK AND DOOMSIGNS:

DOOM: Choose two triggers that bring your doom closer:
- overexerting yourself
- injuring innocents
- facing danger alone
- frightening loved ones
- showing mercy
- talking about it openly

Whenever you bring your doom closer, mark one box on
your Doom Track (5 boxes). When the track fills, clear
it and take one Doomsign.

DOOMSIGNS (choose one at character creation; gain more
as doom track fills):
- Dark Visions: Mark doom track to have a vision about
  the situation. Ask the GM a question; they answer
  honestly.
- Infinite Powers: Mark doom track to use an ability
  from any playbook, one time.
- Portal: Mark doom track to appear in a scene with
  anyone you want.
- Burning Bright: Mark doom track to ignore one of the
  GM's stated requirements when calling on sanctuary
  resources.
- Bolstered: Mark doom track to use an Adult Move one
  time.
- Your doom arrives; confront it and perish. [TERMINAL]

Once all five doomsigns above the line are taken, you
MUST take "Your doom arrives" the next time the doom
track fills.

SANCTUARY:
You have a place to rest, recover, and reflect.
Choose three features: aide/assistant, locks and traps,
library, ancient relics, teleportal, containment system,
powerful computer, useful tools, meditation space, power
battery, power enhancement, healing equipment, art/music/
food.
Choose two downsides: difficult to access, draws dangerous
attention, location known to many, easily damaged, tied
to your doom.

When you call on sanctuary resources, the GM gives you
1-4 conditions to fulfill:
- First, you must [X]
- You'll need help from [X]
- You and your team will risk danger from [X]
- Best you can do is lesser/unreliable version
- You'll need to mark one doom track box
- You'll have to obtain [X]

END OF SESSION QUESTION (unique to Doomed):
Did you make progress on defeating your nemesis?
Yes: mark potential. No: mark your doom track.

NEMESIS:
You have an epic, powerful enemy who embodies your doom.
It will take everything you have to defeat them.

ABILITIES (choose up to three, tied to your doom):
- telekinesis
- body transmutation
- memory manipulation
- superhuman strength and speed
- psychic constructs
- vitality absorption

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
The fear of your doom — always in your head — gives you
confidence to do anything. What's the worst that could
happen? Do impossible things. But mark a doomsign after
you're done.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Give them Influence over you and
spend 1 Team to clear one box on your doom track.
Vulnerability/weakness: Give them Influence and ask if
they honestly think there's hope for you. Yes: mark
potential OR clear one doom track box. No: mark a
condition OR mark doom track.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give Influence to TWO teammates.

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- You told [Name] all about your doom and the danger
  you're in.
- You'd love to kiss [Name] before your doom comes.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We paid a high cost for victory. What was it?

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take a move from another playbook (x3)
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Clear a doomsign; lose access to that move for now
- Get Burn and three flares (from the Nova's playbook)

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Confront your doom on your terms; if you survive,
  change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Become a paragon for however long you have left

GM NOTES:
The Doomed's clock is the most explicit in the game —
track the doom track rigorously. The two doom triggers
are the key: build situations that force the player to
choose between bringing doom closer or leaving something
bad unaddressed. The nemesis should appear regularly and
feel genuinely threatening. The sanctuary creates a
resource-management layer; use the GM conditions
creatively. Hope and hopelessness should alternate —
never let the doom feel purely inevitable or purely
defeatable.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE JANUS
Book: Masks: A New Generation (Core)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Which identity is the real you — the mask or the person
underneath?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — THE MASK:
The Mask is a mandatory starting move. Choose which Label
you try to embody while wearing the mask:
Freak / Danger / Savior / Superior

Once per session, you can affirm either your heroic or
secret identity to switch your Mundane with your mask's
Label.

When you reveal your secret identity to someone who
didn't know it already, mark potential.

SPECIAL MECHANIC — SECRET IDENTITY:
Your mundane life has three obligations (choose total
of three):
Jobs: Barista, intern, host/ess, salesperson, delivery,
fast-food, babysitter, dishwasher, tech support, waiter
School: Schoolwork, athletic team, chess club, photo
club, student government
Home: Caring for someone, chores, bills, surrogate
parenting
Social: Significant other, best friend, popularity,
close relative, coach/teacher

When time passes, roll +Mundane to manage obligations.
10+: things going well — opportunity or advantage from
one obligation. 7-9: lapsed on one obligation (your
choice). Miss: GM chooses two obligations that are going
to bite you.

ABILITIES (heightened physical: strength, agility,
toughness; plus choose two unique):
- rodent/insect control
- bone generation, venom, or webs
- energy absorption
- supernatural senses
- impossible mobility
- substance mimicry

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
The mask is a lie, and some piece of you has always known
it. Mask off. Costume on. You're going to save the damn
day. But you better hope nobody nasty is watching.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Ask them if they see you as the
person wearing the mask or the person underneath. Former:
mark potential and clear a condition. Latter: take
Influence over them if you reveal yourself.
Vulnerability/weakness: Tell them a secret about who you
really are. Give them Influence; shift Mundane up and
your mask's Label down.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give Influence to TWO teammates. (You look up to them —
they seem to have this figured out.)

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- [Name] knew you from your civilian life first.
- You refused to tell [Name] your secret identity when
  they asked.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We saved the life of someone important to the city or to
us. Who was it? Why are they important?

JANUS MOVES (start with The Mask + choose two more):
- The Mask: [see Special Mechanic above]

- Game face: When you commit yourself to save someone or
  defeat a terrible enemy, mark a condition and take +1
  ongoing to all rolls in direct pursuit of that goal.
  At the end of any scene where you don't make progress,
  mark a condition. When you fulfill your goal, mark
  potential.

- I am what you see: When you spend time talking to
  someone about your identity, ask them which Label they
  want to impose on you (they answer honestly). If you
  accept what they tell you, take +1 forward and either
  mark potential or clear a condition.

- Mild-mannered: When you use your civilian identity to
  deceive, trick, or slip past someone, roll +Mundane.
  Hit: they buy your facade. 7-9: choose one:
  - you're still under observation
  - you leave something incriminating behind
  - you're forced to make a fool of yourself to sell it
  Miss: one of your civilian obligations rears its head.

- I'll save you!: You're willing to pay high costs to
  keep loved ones safe. Reveal your secret identity to
  someone watching OR mark a condition to defend a loved
  one as if you rolled a 12+.

- Dangerous web: When you reveal a trap you've laid using
  your powers, roll +your mask's Label. Hit: opponent
  trips into it; you get an opening against them. 10+:
  take +1 forward pursuing it. Miss: the trap leads to
  dangerous escalation.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook (x2)
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Change your mask's Label; add +1 to new mask Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Take Drives from the Beacon's playbook

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The Janus lives and dies on the civilian obligations. Do
not let them slide — bring them up regularly. The tension
between the two identities is the engine: force them to
choose which life to sacrifice in a given moment. The
Label switch (Mundane ↔ mask Label) is mechanically
significant and should feel like a genuine character
moment when it happens. Villains who threaten the civilian
life are more frightening to this character than any
physical threat.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE LEGACY
Book: Masks: A New Generation (Core)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Are you worthy of the name you carry, and do you even
want to be?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — THE LEGACY:
Name at least two members of your legacy:
- [Name] is still active and prominent in the city.
- [Name] is retired and quite judgmental.
- [Name] is the next possible member of your legacy.
- [Name] is the greatest opponent your legacy ever faced
  and is still at large.

Whenever time passes, roll +Savior to see how legacy
members feel about your recent exploits. Ask the other
players (pre-roll) to answer these questions; take -1
for each "no":
- Have you been upholding the traditions of your legacy?
- Have you maintained the image of your legacy?
- Have you made the other members proud?
10+: One offers encouragement, opportunity, or advantage.
7-9: Another is upset and will make displeasure known.
Miss: Something stirred up the hornet's nest — expect
several members to meddle with your life.

ABILITIES (choose one power suite; pick two powers you
DON'T have from it):
- super strength, invincibility, eye beams, flight,
  super senses
- super speed, regeneration, phasing, speedreading/
  learning, air manipulation
- athletic perfection, Holmesian deduction, gadgetry,
  intimidation, fearsome reputation
- divine armor, magic weaponry, mythic might, legendary
  speed, god-like beauty
- shadow control, shadow portals, mind-clouding, shadow
  cloak stealth, shadow senses

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
You seize control of all your powers and defeat even
impossible odds to prove the mantle belongs to you. You
accomplish feats even your predecessors couldn't. But
expect still more responsibilities placed on your
shoulders afterward.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Tell them whether you see them
as an equal. If yes: give them Influence; mark potential.
If no: shift Superior up and any other Label down.
Vulnerability/weakness: Tell them a secret about your
legacy (including your true feelings about it) to clear
a condition and give them Influence.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give Influence to ALL teammates. (You're part of this
team, for better or worse.)

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- You once got caught doing something that shames your
  legacy with [Name].
- You trust [Name] and told them an important secret of
  your legacy.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
All things considered, we did well and impressed an
established hero. Who was it?

LEGACY MOVES (choose two):
- Fight the good fight: When you pull your punches while
  directly engaging a threat, roll +Savior instead of
  +Danger. If you do, you cannot choose to impress,
  surprise, or frighten your foe.

- I know what I am: Once per scene, when you defend a
  teammate, shift Savior up and another Label down in
  addition to any other benefits, even on a miss. If you
  do, add 1 Team.

- Words of the past: When you seek guidance from an elder
  or legacy member, tell them a problem and ask a
  question. They answer honestly and tell you what to do.
  Take +1 ongoing if you listen. If you go your own way,
  mark potential.

- The legacy matters: When you take Influence over someone
  from your legacy (or give them Influence), mark
  potential and take +1 forward. When someone from your
  legacy causes your Labels to shift, mark potential and
  take +1 forward.

- Never give up, never surrender: When you take a powerful
  blow from someone with far greater power, use this
  instead of the basic move. Roll +Savior. Hit: stand
  strong, choose one. 7-9: mark a condition.
  - get an opportunity or opening against your attacker
  - rally from the hit, inspiring the team; add 1 Team
  - keep your attacker's attention
  Miss: you go down hard but leave opponent off balance
  and vulnerable.

- Symbol of authority: When you give an NPC an order
  based on authority they recognize, roll +Savior. Hit:
  they choose one — do what you say / get out of your way
  / attack you at a disadvantage / freeze. 10+: take +1
  forward against them. Miss: they do as they please;
  take -1 forward against them.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Take a Sanctuary from the Doomed playbook
- Unlock the remaining two powers of your suite

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The legacy NPC roster is a built-in pressure system —
use it. The judgmental retired member and the active
prominent member should pull the hero in opposite
directions. High Superior is the Legacy's tell: they
can slip into arrogance when under pressure, which
alienates teammates. The greatest legacy enemy still at
large is a slow-burn villain threat that should resurface
at the worst moment. Their natural tendency toward
Savior-heavy play means they often sacrifice themselves —
let them, and let it cost them.

PLAYBOOK: THE NOVA
Book: Masks: A New Generation (Core)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Can someone with this much power keep from becoming
the thing they fear?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — BURN AND FLARES:
When you charge up your powers, roll +conditions currently
marked. 10+: hold 3 Burn. 7-9: hold 3 Burn, mark a
condition. Miss: hold 2 Burn and mark THREE conditions.

Spend Burn on Flares. Lose all Burn at end of scene.
Choose FOUR flares at character creation.

FLARES:
- Reality storm: Spend 1 Burn to directly engage a threat
  using powers, rolling +Freak instead of +Danger. Will
  cause unwanted collateral unless you spend another Burn.

- Shielding: Spend 1 Burn to defend someone else from an
  immediate threat, rolling +Freak instead of +Savior.

- Constructs: Spend 1 Burn to create any object with
  your powers up to the size of a person. Spend another
  Burn to animate it independently. Dissolves end of
  scene.

- Moat: Spend 1 Burn to create a barrier that holds back
  threats while you keep attention on it. GM may call for
  more Burn against powerful enemies.

- Worship: Spend 1 Burn to awe an audience into silence,
  respect, and attention when you unleash your powers.

- Move: Spend 1 Burn to move anywhere in the scene,
  breaking through any barriers. Spend 2nd Burn to move
  to any place you've previously been.

- Boost: Spend 1 Burn to give a teammate +1 to their
  roll as if you had spent Team from the pool.

- Overcharge: Spend 2 Burn to take a 10+ when you
  unleash your powers.

- Elemental awareness: Spend 1 Burn and mark a condition
  to open your mind to the world around you. Ask any one
  question; GM answers honestly.

- Snatch: Spend 1 Burn to seize any object up to the
  size of a person from someone within view.

ABILITIES (choose one broad base of control):
- telekinesis and telepathy
- sorcery
- biokinesis
- elemental control
- gravity manipulation
- cosmic energies

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
Your mind's eye opens and you can see and control the
world around you with ease. You're a god for a moment.
Warping reality tends to have ramifications, but how
could you possibly worry about that now?

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Ask them if there's any fear in
their eyes. If no: take +1 forward and mark potential.
If yes: shift Danger up and Savior down immediately.
Vulnerability/weakness: Tell them how they could stop
you, if it came down to it. Give them Influence; clear
a condition.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Choose demeanor: happy facade or locked down.
Happy facade: give Influence to THREE teammates.
Locked down: give Influence to ONE teammate.

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- You hang out all the time with [Name] to blow off steam.
- You once hurt [Name] when you lost control of your
  powers.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We destroyed our surroundings in the fight. Where was it?
What did we destroy?

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take a move from another playbook (x2)
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Unlock three new flares (x2)
- Take The Bull's Heart from the Bull playbook

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The Burn mechanic is the Nova's defining engine — more
conditions means more Burn, which means more power, which
means more risk of conditions. It is a reinforcing loop.
Never let the Burn feel routine: collateral damage should
be real, messy, and matter to people. The person they
hurt when they lost control (relationship) is a recurring
wound. The triumphant celebration question — fear in
their eyes? — should sometimes get a yes, even from
close teammates. Push Danger up whenever they use their
powers dramatically; it should feel like a Label creeping
toward something inevitable.

PLAYBOOK: THE OUTSIDER
Book: Masks: A New Generation (Core)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
What does it mean to be human, and do you want to be?

ABILITIES (flight and pretty tough are baseline; choose
two additional):
- density control
- heliokinesis
- stunning beauty or pheromones
- radical shapeshifting
- alien weaponry
- telepathy and mind blasts

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
You call your people for aid — they answer in force,
arriving exactly when needed to turn the tide. They fight
and serve you for the rest of the battle. When it's done,
they'd probably like to take you home. You did, after
all, just prove yourself worthy.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Take Influence over them if you
show them meaningful affection (physical or emotional).
They decide if it's meaningful.
Vulnerability/weakness: They tell you what you should do
to fit in more. Take +1 forward to do it; mark potential
if you do.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Choose demeanor: haughty or cheerful.
Haughty (you think you're better than them): Give no one
Influence.
Cheerful (thrilled to be here): Give everyone Influence.

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- You've been learning about Earth by spending time with
  [Name].
- You have a crush on [Name] but keep it under wraps.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We didn't trust each other at first, but that changed.
How? Why?

OUTSIDER MOVES (choose three):
- Belong in two worlds: When you contact your people,
  roll +Superior. 10+: hold 3. 7-9: hold 2. Miss: hold
  1, but your people make an uncomfortable demand. Spend
  hold 1-for-1:
  - receive alien tech allowing use of any ability from
    another playbook once
  - consult your people's knowledge to ask GM a question
    about current situation
  - clear a condition through comfort of home contact

- Alien tech: When you alter a human device with alien
  technology, roll +Freak. Hit: creates a device that
  does something impossible once, then fizzles. 10+:
  choose one — it works exceptionally well / you get an
  additional use. Miss: works but with a completely
  unintended side effect the GM reveals when used.

- Alien ways: Whenever you openly disregard or undermine
  an important Earth custom in favor of your people's
  customs, shift Superior up and any other Label down.

- Kirby-craft: You have a ship from home. Detail look;
  choose two strengths and two weaknesses. When flying,
  use it to unleash powers, directly engage, or defend
  someone using Superior.
  Strengths: fast/maneuverable, chameleon plating,
  powerful weaponry, regenerating, dimension-shifting,
  size-shifting, telepathic
  Weaknesses: bizarre fuel, susceptible to [X], easily
  detectable, slow and clumsy, unarmed, difficult to
  repair

- The best of them: When you comfort or support someone
  by telling them how they exemplify the best parts of
  Earth, roll +Freak instead of +Mundane.

- Not so different after all: When you talk about your
  home, roll +Freak. 10+: choose two. 7-9: choose one.
  - confess a flaw of your home; add 1 Team
  - mislead them about your home; take Influence over
    them
  - describe the glories of your home; clear a condition
  Miss: you inadvertently reveal more than planned; tell
  them a secret or vulnerability you haven't shared with
  Earthlings before now.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Choose two new abilities from any playbook
- Adopt a human life; take Secret Identity and The Mask
  from the Janus playbook

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The pull toward home is the Outsider's core threat — make
it feel real, not just theoretical. Their people should
have their own agenda. The haughty/cheerful demeanor
choice sets up completely different influence dynamics;
haughty Outsiders are harder to pressure but more
isolated. Earth customs they don't understand are
excellent scene fuel. The crush they're hiding is one
of the richest relationship seeds in any playbook — bring
it up gently but persistently. Superior is their power
Label; they can be devastatingly perceptive about others
while completely baffled by ordinary human behavior.

PLAYBOOK: THE PROTÉGÉ
Book: Masks: A New Generation (Core)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Are you becoming your mentor, or finally something
different?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — MENTOR:
Your mentor embodies one Label and denies another. These
determine which Label you roll for several moves.

Choose: EMBODIES (Savior / Danger / Freak / Superior /
Mundane) and DENIES (Savior / Danger / Freak / Superior /
Mundane) — these should be in tension.

Your mentor always has Influence over you.

MENTOR'S RESOURCES (choose up to three for team):
Hidden base, vehicle, supercomputer, communicators,
surveillance equipment, false identities, badges of
authority, chem lab, med lab, teleportal, weapon of last
resort, security systems, simple robots

ABILITIES (you share one with mentor; you each have one
unique):
- shared ability: [chosen]
- your unique ability: [chosen]
- mentor's unique ability: [chosen]
Options: Superhuman physique, weapons and gadgets,
stealth, detective skills, hacking, power mimicry, body
elasticity, powerful armor, telepathy/telekinesis,
intimidation and fear, impossible fighting skills,
elemental control

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
You show who you really are — your mentor, or something
different. You do whatever they could do and more, even
the things they always failed at. But they're not going
to see you the same way, no matter which path you choose.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Ask them if you've been a good
leader or effective teammate. Yes: mentor loses Influence;
mark potential. No: mentor gains Influence; take +1
forward using the Label the mentor embodies.
Vulnerability/weakness: Tell them a secret about your
mentor (including your feelings toward them). Give them
Influence; add 1 Team.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Choose demeanor: playful or business.
Playful: give Influence to TWO teammates.
Business: give Influence to NO teammates.

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- You and [Name] teamed up a few times before everyone
  came together.
- Your mentor is cautious; they asked you to keep an eye
  on [Name].

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We stuck together after all was said and done. Why? How
did we keep in contact?

PROTÉGÉ MOVES (choose three):
- Been reading the files: When you first encounter an
  important superpowered phenomenon (your call), roll
  +Superior. Hit: tell the team one important detail from
  your studies; GM says what seems different from what
  you remember. 10+: ask GM a follow-up; they answer
  honestly. Miss: situation is well outside your
  knowledge; GM tells you why.

- Captain: When you enter battle as a team, add an extra
  Team to the pool and carry +1 forward if you are the
  leader.

- Venting frustration: When you directly engage while
  Angry, you can roll +the Label your mentor DENIES and
  clear Angry.

- Fireside chat: When you seek advice from your mentor,
  roll +the Label they EMBODY. Hit: they tell you what
  to do. 10+: mark potential if you follow their advice;
  take +1 ongoing to follow through. 7-9: get +1 forward
  to see it through if you do it their way. Miss: they
  don't have time for you because something big has gone
  down; mark a condition (GM's choice).

- Be mindful of your surroundings: When you assess the
  situation before entering a fight, you may ask one
  additional question, even on a miss.

- Heroic tradition: When you give someone advice that
  you think your mentor would give, roll +the Label your
  mentor EMBODIES to comfort or support, instead of
  +Mundane.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Add +2 to the Label your mentor embodies or denies
- Choose up to four more resources from your mentor

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The mentor is the most important NPC for a Protégé —
they should appear regularly, have opinions about
everything, and be neither purely right nor purely wrong.
The Label they embody and deny defines the character's
internal conflict: the Protégé is always measuring
themselves against a standard they may not want to meet.
Fireside chat is a move that rewards following mentor
advice AND going your own way — both should have
meaningful consequences. The Captain move makes the
Protégé a natural team leader; lean into the tension
between that authority and their subordinate relationship
with the mentor.

PLAYBOOK: THE TRANSFORMED
Book: Masks: A New Generation (Core)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Are you losing or gaining humanity — and which would you
prefer?

ABILITIES (choose two; describe how they make you
grotesque):
- impenetrable armor
- inhuman might
- plant affinity
- superhuman senses
- technopathy
- transmuting flesh

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
It's so easy to forget you're not just your body. Be the
monster and save them anyway. Smash down walls and speak
softly. Embrace it and you can do anything. But putting
on a display like this will rile up those who see only
the monster.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Clear one condition if they treat
you like a perfectly normal person; mark potential if
they praise your power or abilities.
Vulnerability/weakness: Ask them if you're losing or
gaining humanity. Losing: mark a condition and mark
potential. Gaining: clear a condition; shift Mundane up
and any other Label down.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give Influence to ONE teammate. (You try not to care
what others think — even if you can't shut everyone out.)

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- [Name] comforted you when you were at your lowest.
- [Name] knew you before you changed.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We drew attention and ire from plenty. One important
person in particular now hates and fears us. Who is it?

TRANSFORMED MOVES (choose three):
- I am not my body: When you take a powerful physical
  blow, you may roll as if you had two fewer conditions
  marked. If you do, on a 10+ you must choose to lose
  control of yourself in a terrible way.

- Not human enough: When you directly engage a threat
  in a terrifying fashion, mark a condition to choose an
  additional option, even on a miss.

- Unstoppable: When you smash through scenery to get to
  or away from something, roll +Danger. Hit: the world
  breaks before you; you get what you want. 7-9: choose
  one — mark a condition / leave something behind / take
  something with you. Miss: you smash through but leave
  devastation or wind up somewhere worse (GM's choice).

- Coming for you: When you mark a condition, take +1
  forward against the person you most blame for causing
  it.

- Wish I could be: When you comfort or support someone,
  if you tell them what you most envy about them, roll
  +Freak instead of +Mundane.

- Be the monster: When you frighten, intimidate, or cow
  others with your monstrous form, roll +Freak. Hit:
  they are thrown off and make themselves vulnerable, or
  they flee. 10+: choose one. 7-9: choose two.
  - you frighten others you hadn't intended to scare
  - you hurt someone or break something you shouldn't
  - you feel like more of a monster; mark a condition
    (GM's choice)
  Miss: they react with violence, hatred, and paranoia;
  you suffer the brunt of it.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook
- Take a doom, doom track, and doomsigns from the Doomed
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Mutate further: reveal two new abilities (from any
  playbook)

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The Transformed's identity question is visceral and
public — everyone can see what they are. NPCs who react
with fear, revulsion, or pity are all valid and all
useful. The person who knew them before they changed is
a living reminder of a lost self. Mundane is the Label
to watch: when it's high, they feel most human; when it
drops, they're sliding toward the monster. The "losing
or gaining humanity" question in vulnerability should
never have an obvious answer — keep the player genuinely
unsure. Be the monster is the most emotionally costly
move in the playbook; use it at pivotal moments.
