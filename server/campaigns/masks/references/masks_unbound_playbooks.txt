MASKS: UNBOUND — PLAYBOOK REFERENCE
For use in Section 13 of the Solo GM Prompt
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
3 playbooks: The Harbinger, The Nomad, The Scion.
NOTE: These three playbooks are more mechanically
demanding than core playbooks and are best introduced
once the campaign is established. The Nomad in
particular has a completely overhauled Influence system
(Putting Down Roots) that requires careful tracking.
If any of these are used for Claude-played teammates,
introduce them at session 3 or later, not at session
zero.
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE HARBINGER
Book: Masks: Unbound
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Can you change the future, or does trying to change it
only make it worse?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — CONNECTING THE DOTS:
Your memories of the future are scrambled. You are
piecing together how the present becomes the future
you know.

The Connecting the Dots roster has six named roles.
Each name you fill in raises your Memories score
by 1 (max +3, starting at -1):
- Monster: ___________
- Traitor: ___________
- Corruptor: ___________
- Martyr: ___________
- Architect: ___________
- Leader: ___________

CONNECTING THE DOTS RULES:
- When you write a name above: they gain Influence over
  you if they didn't already have it.
- When you push yourself to remember the version of
  someone that exists in your future: mark a condition
  and roll +Memories. Hit: you connect who they are now
  to who they are in the future; choose the role they
  fulfill in the future, and the GM tells you about their
  future self. 10+: ask a follow-up question. Miss:
  they're not at all who you thought; the GM chooses
  their role, or tells you they don't exist in the future.
- At the start of each session OR when time passes:
  roll +Savior to find out how your timeline
  investigations are going. Hit: choose one figure noted
  above or one aspect of the future world — you've found
  a lead to learn how they became their future version.
  10+: the lead is particularly strong; ask the GM one
  question about that figure or aspect; they answer
  honestly. Miss: you're lost in the present; the GM
  tells you how things are so different here and shifts
  your Labels according to how it makes you feel.

MOMENT OF TRUTH SPECIAL RULE:
After using your Moment of Truth, RESET ALL NAMES in
your Connecting the Dots section. You've changed enough
of the timeline to invalidate your prior research.

ABILITIES (your powers may have something to do with
how you traveled back; choose one):
- super speed
- optimized biology
- telepathy and telekinesis
- teleportation and portals
- futuristic gadgets and implants
- energy absorption/redirection

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
In this moment, you can see the course of events laid
out like a river — you know exactly what you have to do
to ensure the future outcome you want. After this, you've
changed enough of the timeline to invalidate your prior
research. Reset all names in Connecting the Dots.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Confess to a teammate the
difference between what you thought they would be and
what they really are. Add 1 Team. Ask them if you
represent a future they'd want. Yes: mark potential.
No: mark a condition.
Vulnerability/weakness (share a tale of future loss):
Ask if they think you have the ability to avert that
loss. If yes: mark potential and give them Influence.
If no: mark Guilty and shift one Label up and one down
(your choice). If Guilty is already marked, mark another
condition (GM's choice).

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give Influence to ALL teammates. (Every member of your
team has the power to change the future — your future.)

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- [Name] turns away from the hero's path, according to
  your history books. You have to prevent that from
  happening.
- You've always idolized [Name], but it's too awkward
  to admit it to them now that you've met them. Keep it
  cool, keep it cool.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We averted a disaster from the future's history books.
What was the disaster? What effect do we hope it had
on the timeline?

HARBINGER MOVES (choose two):
- Stick to the mission: When you defend someone or
  something critically important to the future (tell us
  why), you may mark a condition to shift down one Label
  and shift up Savior before the roll. If you do, you
  cannot choose the option "clear a condition" for the
  defend move.

- Tomorrow's golden promise: When you comfort or support
  someone with tales of what you think their future will
  be, you may roll +Savior instead of +Mundane. Mark a
  condition unless you are confident the stories are
  true.

- Applied history: When you pierce the mask of someone
  you believe will shape the future, roll +Savior instead
  of +Mundane.

- You haven't learned you can do that yet: When you tell
  a teammate about a new, never-before-performed trick
  they've never considered, spend a Team from the pool
  to allow them to unleash their powers, directly engage
  a threat, or defend someone using YOUR Superior as if
  it were their Label. Any costs or complications from
  their move affect you as well.

- I learned the solution in grade school: When you
  unleash your powers to extend your senses or overcome
  an intellectual obstacle, you may roll +Superior
  instead of +Freak.

- 21st century studies: When you assess the situation,
  you may always ask "what does the future know about
  this moment?", even on a miss. You take +1 ongoing to
  act on the answers.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook (x2)
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label (x2)
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Return to the future and accept its new form, or jump
  to a different point in the past to begin mission anew

GM NOTES:
The Connecting the Dots mechanic is the most GM-
intensive system in the Unbound playbooks. Track the
roster carefully — every name added is a new Influence
relationship AND a new story thread. The Memories score
rising from -1 toward +3 represents the Harbinger
gradually piecing together a picture that may be very
different from what they expected. The "lost in the
present" miss on the session-start roll is a license to
shift Labels dramatically and reorient the character.
The Moment of Truth reset is genuinely significant — all
those carefully built connections vanish. Use it at a
dramatically appropriate moment, not just when the
player wants to. The idolized-teammate relationship is
enormous emotional fuel; the teammate they're trying to
keep on the hero's path should be present in most
difficult scenes.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE NOMAD
Book: Masks: Unbound
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Can you ever truly commit to one place, one people,
one cause?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — PUTTING DOWN ROOTS:
The Nomad has a fundamentally different Influence system
from every other playbook. Track all of the following
rules precisely — they override the standard Influence
rules for this character only.

PUTTING DOWN ROOTS RULES:
- Adults do NOT have Influence over you by default.
  No one does at character creation.
- You can only give a total of 6 Influence (lifetime
  maximum during campaign).
- During play, you can only give Influence by revealing
  a vulnerability or weakness to someone. You CAN still
  give Influence through the end-of-session move. You
  CANNOT give Influence to someone who already has
  Influence over you.
- Others CANNOT take Influence over you. If they would,
  instead they choose: mark potential OR inflict a
  condition on you.
- You reject Influence at -2 by default (instead of
  the standard +0).
- When someone takes advantage of their Influence over
  you, they can choose TWO options (instead of the
  standard one).
- At the end of a session, you can take back 1 Influence
  from someone you choose.
- If you have given out 0 Influence: you cannot comfort
  or support anyone. If you would trigger that move,
  instead mark a condition as you say exactly the wrong
  thing. If someone tries to comfort or support you, you
  cannot open up to them.

PUTTING DOWN ROOTS — STACKING BENEFITS (based on total
Influence given out; these stack):
- 1-2 Influence given: When you defend someone who has
  Influence over you, you can ignore the Insecure
  condition. When you directly engage someone who has
  Influence over you, you can ignore the Afraid
  condition.
- 3 Influence given: When you take a powerful blow from
  someone with Influence over you, take -2 to the roll.
- 4 Influence given: When you pierce the mask of someone
  who has Influence over you, you can always ask them
  one question, even on a miss.
- 5 Influence given: When you spend a Team to help
  someone who has Influence over you, it gives them +2.
- 6 Influence given: When you accept the words of
  someone who has Influence over you, mark potential,
  clear a condition, or take +1 forward.

ABILITIES (tech and tools picked up during travels;
choose two):
- rift generator
- anti-grav pack
- forcefield projectors
- cloaking rig
- modular blaster
- wearable ultraprocessor

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
You pour everything you have and everything you are into
this moment. You pull off tricks no one from this planet
has seen before. You devote yourself, here and now, to
a cause, and you achieve your goal in ways you never
could have if you'd only stayed home. But now you've
proved to everyone that you really don't belong here —
and the very skills that let you succeed are the ones
you earned out there.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Ask them what they admire about
you. The GM shifts one of your Labels up and one down
based on what they say.
Vulnerability/weakness: Mark a condition, give them
Influence over you, and mark potential.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give NO ONE Influence. (You're here, but not, and it's
going to take time before you really commit.)

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- [Name] knows plenty that I don't about this world, its
  people, and how to deal with them; I should follow
  their lead while I'm here.
- [Name] is... well... boring. But that's okay! I'm
  going to make them more interesting.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
We had to deal with a danger I'd met while traveling the
farscapes. What was it, and how did we send it away?

NOMAD MOVES (choose two):
- No good at being a kid: When you clear a condition,
  you can shift Labels, your choice.

- Watched C-beams glitter: When you make a claim about
  a strange situation or phenomenon based on your
  experience, roll +Freak. Hit: you were right; the GM
  tells you what opportunity your knowledge grants. 7-9:
  your claim is incomplete, flawed, or tangential; the
  GM tells you how. Miss: your experiences are
  incomplete; the GM tells you what mistake your flawed
  understanding leads you to make.

- I hate calling the cavalry: When you call the allies
  you made beyond this world to ask them for help, mark
  a condition and roll +Superior. Hit: someone shows up
  to lend a hand. 7-9: they're dragging their own
  problems. Miss: your call reaches someone you wish it
  hadn't.

- The littlest space bandit: When you directly engage a
  threat that underestimates you, roll +Superior instead
  of +Danger.

- Out of this world: When you plug into the cosmic
  aether and tune out of this world, roll +Freak. Hit:
  you come to a new, interesting insight about your
  current problems; the GM tells you what. 10+: you can
  also shift your own Labels to match your new
  understanding. Miss: you realize something dangerous
  or terrible; the GM tells you what and shifts your
  Labels accordingly.

- Sharpened eyes: When you assess the situation, you may
  always ask one of the following, even on a miss:
  - What's my best way out/way in/way past?
  - What here would be handy to grab?
  - Who here is susceptible to my words?

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x3)
- Take a move from another playbook (x2)
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label (x2)

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Depart for reaches unknown, never to return

GM NOTES:
The Putting Down Roots system is the most unusual
Influence mechanic in the game. At the start of a
campaign the Nomad is almost entirely closed off — no
one has Influence, rejection is at -2, adults have no
automatic hold. The arc of the playbook is the slow
accumulation of Influence given out, which unlocks
stacking benefits. Do not rush this. Each Influence
given should feel significant. The "no one does"
starting state means that for early sessions, attempts
to use Influence on the Nomad simply don't work the
normal way — track this and remind the player when
it's relevant. The boring-teammate relationship is
intentionally asymmetrical and often becomes the most
important relationship in the campaign. The "depart
for reaches unknown, never to return" terminal advance
should feel like a real possibility from session one.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PLAYBOOK: THE SCION
Book: Masks: Unbound
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CORE DRAMATIC QUESTION:
Can you be someone different from what you were born
to be, and can you make the world believe it?

SPECIAL MECHANIC — YOUR LINEAGE:
You are the child of a true supervillain. Define your
parent at character creation:

What kind of villain are they? (circle at least one):
deadly mercenary / master thief / hateful destroyer /
zealous crusader / cruel mastermind / grand tyrant

What is your relationship with them like? (circle one):
mostly strangers / actively adversarial / sadly opposed /
ever-shifting / redemption-corruption arc / full of
denial

Their abilities, assets, and strengths (choose up to 3):
superhuman martial prowess / dark sorcery / cosmic might
/ diabolical machines / endless minions / unrivaled
genius / powerful allies / arsenal of weapons and
gadgets / supremely honed skills

What abilities do you use to fight them? (choose up to 2):
impressive martial prowess / magical aptitude / cosmic
energies / machine control / regeneration /
electrokinesis / seismic waves / a powerful weapon or
gadget / mastery of a single skillset

SPECIAL MECHANIC — RESPECT:
Write down the names of at least two characters whose
respect you need to earn to differentiate yourself from
your parent. Fill in new names whenever appropriate.

Respect track slots (each has 4 boxes + 1 Advancement
box):
- Your parent's greatest enemy
- Your parent's greatest victim
- Your personal idol
- The city's greatest leader
- The city's greatest hero
- The city's biggest celebrity

RESPECT RULES:
- When you seek out one of the characters named above:
  roll +Savior. Hit: you track them down. 7-9: they're
  juggling their own problems and may not have time for
  you. Miss: when you find them, the situation is dire;
  the GM tells you how.
- If you earn Influence over a listed character: instead
  mark 1 Respect on them.
- If you lose Influence over a listed character: lose
  1 Respect. If you lose 1 Respect and have none marked,
  immediately mark a condition (GM's choice).
- The first time you reach 4 Respect on an individual:
  take an advancement.
- While you have 4 Respect on an individual:
  - You can reject their Influence at +3.
  - You take +1 to a Label of their choice (record it
    next to their name).
- If you dip below 4 Respect on an individual, you lose
  those benefits.

ABILITIES (choose up to 2 from lineage setup above —
what abilities you use to fight your parent):
[Defined during character creation via lineage setup]

MOMENT OF TRUTH:
The darkness is in you — so you're not fighting it,
you're embracing it. Both hero and villain, and greater
besides. You overcome impossible odds in ways no hero
would approve of and no villain could comprehend. After
this, the rest of the world isn't going to forget who
you really are.

TEAM MOVES:
Triumphant celebration: Ask them if you have earned
their respect. If yes: take Influence over them and
mark potential. If no: give them Influence over you and
mark potential.
Vulnerability/weakness: Ask if they would defend you
against those who mistrust you. If yes: clear a
condition; shift Savior up and any other Label down.
If no: mark a condition; shift Danger up and any other
Label down.

STARTING INFLUENCE:
Give Influence to TWO teammates. (This team may be the
key to proving you're different from your parent, but
you don't want to be defined by your peers either.)

STARTING RELATIONSHIPS:
- [Name] is the only one who understands what I'm going
  through; I told them which part of me I wished was
  more like my parent.
- My parent once fought and thrashed [Name]; I've got
  to find a way to make it up to them.

WHEN OUR TEAM FIRST CAME TOGETHER:
Someone important learned about my lineage and condemned
us all. Who? Why?

SCION MOVES (choose two):
- I'll show them: When you defend someone who doesn't
  believe in you, you can always take Influence over
  them, even on a miss.

- Changed sides: When you mislead or trick an enemy by
  pretending to be on their side, roll +Danger. Hit:
  they buy your charade for now. 7-9: choose 1. 10+:
  choose 2.
  - you avoid having to provide concrete evidence
  - you create an opportunity
  - you expose a weakness or flaw
  Miss: someone else watching comes to the worst possible
  conclusion and acts on it.

- They don't deserve forgiveness: When you accuse an
  enemy of being irredeemable, you can mark two
  conditions to take Influence over you away from them.
  When you directly engage someone who has no Influence
  over you, you can always choose 1 additional option,
  even on a miss.

- Moldable: When you pierce the mask of someone whose
  respect you crave, you can always ask "How could I
  gain Influence over you?", even on a miss. Take +1
  ongoing to acting on the answer.

- All the best stuff: You've compiled access to caches
  of equipment and weaponry other supers have hidden in
  the city. When you access a cache, say whose it is.
  If it's a hero's: roll +Savior. If it's a villain's:
  roll +Danger. Hit: you find a tool or intel useful to
  your situation; the GM details it. 7-9: you leave
  evidence you've been here. Miss: you tripped an alarm
  and they're coming; prepare to explain yourself.

- White lies: When you comfort or support someone by
  telling them lies they want to hear, roll +Savior
  instead of +Mundane. On a hit, if they open up to you,
  take Influence over them.

ADVANCEMENT (first 5):
- Take another move from your playbook (x2)
- Take a move from another playbook (x2)
- Unlock your Moment of Truth
- Someone permanently loses Influence; add +1 to a Label
- Rearrange Labels as you choose; add +1 to a Label
- Take The Mask and a secret identity from the Janus
  playbook

FURTHER ADVANCEMENT (after 5):
- Unlock Moment of Truth after it's been used once
- Change playbooks
- Take an adult move (x2)
- Lock a Label; add +1 to a Label of your choice
- Retire from the life or become a paragon of the city

GM NOTES:
The Respect system creates a web of specific named NPCs
who are the Scion's entire arc — track it like a
scoreboard. The parent is the most important NPC in
this playbook and should appear regularly: their shadow
falls over every scene even when they're not present.
Crucially, the parent's relationship type (adversarial,
ever-shifting, etc.) defines how that shadow feels.
The "which part of me I wished was more like my parent"
relationship is the most psychologically loaded setup
in any playbook — do not let it stay theoretical; find
moments to press on it. Danger rising is the signal
that the Scion is sliding toward their parent's path;
Savior rising is the signal they're building their own.
The vulnerability/weakness team move is uniquely binary:
does this person have your back or not? The answer
should change over time and should never feel settled.
